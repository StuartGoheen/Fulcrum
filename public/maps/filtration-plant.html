Location Brief — Water Filtration Plant

Far shore of Nymeve Lake, Takodana. An industrial water treatment facility built during the Clone Wars to service a Republic forward operating base that no longer exists. The base was dismantled. The plant was forgotten. The jungle took it back. Now it sits in the swamp like a rusted skeleton — pipes and tanks and concrete slowly dissolving into the green. The only signs of recent habitation are a light in the maintenance shed and track marks in the mud at the southern gate.

The Approach

The swamp thickens as you push past the encampment's edge. Gnarled trees hang with curtains of moss. The ground turns soft, then wet. Insects swarm in clouds around every step. After twenty minutes of trudging through knee-deep muck, the trees open onto a clearing dominated by a rusted industrial structure — the old water filtration plant. Pipes jut from the walls at odd angles, crusted with mineral deposits. Most of the windows are dark. But through a crack in one of the maintenance shed shutters on the west side, you catch the faint glow of a charge lamp. Something moves behind the shutter. A shadow. Then silence.

The Facility

Three circular settling tanks dominate the facility — massive open basins, their water surfaces thick with fluorescent green algae. Rusted catwalks span them at varying heights, some sagging, some collapsed entirely. A grid of rectangular treatment pools fills the eastern quarter, separated by walkways barely wide enough for one person. Pipes run everywhere — overhead, underfoot, through walls — some still hissing faintly with trapped pressure. The jungle has breached the perimeter on all sides. Root systems crack through walls. Vines curtain the openings. The air is thick, humid, and smells of mineral rot and stagnant water.

Difficult Terrain

Most of the facility is difficult terrain. The treatment pool walkways are slick with moss and single-file only. The settling tank catwalks are narrow and unstable. The pipe corridors force single-file movement. The mud around the perimeter is ankle-deep. Only the maintenance shed interior and the processing block floor (despite debris) offer stable footing.

Collapse Risk

The upper catwalk on the primary settling tank sags at the midpoint — more than two people on it at once risks collapse (Coordination to avoid falling into chest-deep water). The secondary tank's catwalk has already partially fallen. Sections of the processing block roof are unstable — a heavy impact or explosion above could bring debris down.

Steam Hazards

Some pipes still carry pressure — trapped gas and residual steam from geothermal seepage. A deliberate rupture (Tech check) or a stray blaster bolt hitting the right junction releases a burst of scalding steam: area denial, partial concealment, and burn damage to anyone caught in the cloud. The GM should mark which pipe junctions are live — not all of them are.

Concealment vs Cover

The jungle vegetation offers concealment but not hard cover — blaster bolts punch through vines and leaves. The concrete pool walls, pipe housings, and the processing block walls provide actual cover. The discharge channel offers both concealment and cover but at the cost of speed. The settling tank catwalks offer no cover at all — anyone on them is silhouetted.

The Sentry

At the southern gate, something moves in a slow, mechanical pattern. Back and forth across the muddy approach, treads carving deep ruts in the soft ground. It's a droid — but not a combat model. An old utility unit, industrial-class, the kind built to haul pipes and clear debris on construction sites. Someone has modified it: crude armor plating welded to its chassis, a cutting torch jury-rigged to swing like a weapon, photoreceptors wired to a proximity sensor that sweeps the gate approach in a stuttering arc. It moves with the graceless determination of a machine following simple instructions: patrol, detect, attack. There is nothing sophisticated about it. That's what makes it dangerous — it doesn't negotiate, it doesn't hesitate, and it doesn't stop until something breaks it.
GM Note — Raden's Sentry Droid: This is a GNK-series utility chassis that Raden salvaged from the facility and spent days rewiring into a crude perimeter alarm. It's not a real combat droid — it's a construction tool with a cutting torch and some scrap welded on. It patrols the southern gate on a fixed loop because that's all Raden could program it to do. It serves two purposes for Raden: it discourages casual scavengers, and the sound of it engaging tells him someone's coming. The droid is slow, predictable, and tough (heavy chassis, improvised armor) but not smart. It can be avoided, disabled (Tech or Ion damage), lured away, or simply destroyed. The Trandoshan hit squad will deal with it quickly when they arrive — it's not meant to stop professionals. It's meant to wake Raden up in time to grab his blaster.

Sensory Details — Quick Reference

Smell

Stagnant water. Mineral rot. The sharp chemical tang of corroded pipe sealant. Wet vegetation and swamp mud. Underneath, the faint ozone burn of a power cell being drained slowly — Raden's charge lamp running on a dying battery.

Sound

Insect drone — constant, layered, rising and falling. The drip of water through pipe joints. The faint hiss of pressure in sealed conduits. The slow mechanical grind of the sentry droid's treads at the south gate, audible from anywhere in the facility. Occasionally: the splash of something large moving in the settling tanks. Swamp fauna.

Temperature

Humid. Oppressively humid. The air doesn't move under the canopy. The concrete and metal hold moisture and radiate damp heat. The maintenance shed is marginally drier — Raden sealed the gaps with foam. The settling tanks are cooler at water level but the mist rising off them makes visibility worse.

Light

Dim green filtered through the jungle canopy. The settling tanks have a faint bioluminescent glow from the algae — enough to see shapes, not enough to read by. The maintenance shed's charge lamp is the brightest point in the facility. The processing block interior is dark — no windows intact, roof partially collapsed lets in shafts of green-filtered daylight. At night, the facility is nearly pitch black except for the algae glow and the sentry droid's stuttering photoreceptor sweep.

GM Note — Combat Spacing: The facility is a defender's nightmare and an ambusher's dream. Multiple approach vectors from the jungle. Narrow corridors between machinery force single-file movement. The settling tank catwalks are exposed killing grounds — anyone on them is silhouetted and has no cover. The maintenance shed is defensible (one reinforced door, small windows) but a dead end if surrounded. The treatment pool walkways create a maze that rewards knowledge of the layout — Raden knows it, the Trandoshans don't (but they have scent-tracking). The pipe network provides concealment and chokepoints but limits movement speed. The discharge channel is the best escape route — concealed, covered, but slow. The processing block's upper gantry offers the best sightlines in the facility but reaching it under fire means climbing a rusted ladder in the open.
