Location Brief — The Sinkhole

Switch's lair. A subterranean bunker buried beneath the Jakku badlands outside Reestkii, accessed through a camouflaged hatch in the desert floor. The locals call the depression above it "the Sinkhole" — a natural formation that Switch has converted into a fortified compound over decades of operation. One way in. One way out. Ceiling-mounted security turrets. And a protocol droid who hasn't had a memory wipe since before the Clone Wars, sitting behind a wall of terminals, running cost-benefit analysis on your life.

The Approach

Maya leads you into the badlands — dune fields and cracked hardpan stretching to the horizon under Jakku's merciless sky. An hour's walk from Reestkii, she stops at what looks like nothing: a shallow depression in the sand, maybe thirty meters across, with a scrap-metal hatch cover half-buried at its center. She knocks twice, pauses, knocks three times. A mechanical whir. The hatch grinds open, revealing a steep metal ramp descending into darkness. Cool air rises — the first cool thing you've felt since landing on this rock. "Don't touch anything," Maya says. "Don't threaten anyone. And whatever you do, don't lie to him. He'll know."

Interior — The Bunker

The ramp drops you into a single large chamber carved from the sandstone, reinforced with welded durasteel beams and salvaged hull plating. The ceiling is low — maybe three meters — held up by scavenged structural ribs from something that used to fly. Green-painted steam pipes run along both walls, hissing softly, feeding heat from a buried reactor somewhere deeper. The air smells like machine oil, ozone, and old metal. Droid parts. Everywhere. Stacked in towering columns along both walls — torsos, limbs, photoreceptors, motivators — decades of scavenging organized by a mind that never forgets and never sleeps. The stacks lean inward like canyon walls, creating narrow corridors on either side of the main floor. At the center: a heavy metal table, bolted to the floor. Chairs around it. This is where business happens. And at the far end, on an elevated platform accessed by metal steps, a bank of computer terminals glows blue-white in the amber dimness. Cable bundles snake from the consoles into the walls. And on the platform, standing perfectly still, watching you with photoreceptors that haven't blinked in forty years — Switch. He doesn't greet you. He waits. He's been waiting longer than you've been alive.

Construction

Natural sandstone sinkhole, reinforced with welded durasteel beams and scavenged starship hull plating. The structural ribs in the ceiling are from a crashed vessel — Clone Wars era, by the markings. The floor is raw sandstone patched with metal plates where the stone cracked. Power comes from a buried reactor core deeper underground, feeding the terminals, turrets, lighting, and the steam system through the pipe network.

Scale

Single chamber, roughly 15m long (north-south) by 10m wide (east-west). Ceiling height approximately 3m. The elevated command platform at the north end adds another meter of height advantage. The scrap corridors on either side narrow to about 2m width. Cramped enough that a Chevin fills the entry hatch and a brawl involves everyone. Roughly 16 combatants in this space during the Ganga Lor assault.

The Command Platform

Elevated metal platform at the north end, accessed by short steps on either side. Banks of computer terminals, holographic data displays, a code cylinder reader built into the main console. This is where Switch cracks the cylinder, where he conducts business, where he watches everything from above. The platform provides height advantage and clear sightlines across the entire room — and it's flanked by two ceiling-mounted security turrets.

The Negotiation Table

Heavy durasteel table, bolted to the sandstone floor. Not going anywhere. Surrounded by mismatched chairs — some metal, some scavenged furniture, one that looks like it came from a Lambda-class shuttle. The table surface is scored with knife marks and blaster scoring. Datapads, tools, and drink containers are scattered across it. During the fight, the table provides solid cover from the entry ramp but leaves flanks exposed to the corridors.

The Assault — Ganga Lor's Breach

The bunker's motion sensors scream. Before you can react, the hatch above explodes inward. Standing in the smoking frame is a massive Chevin — Ganga Lor, Varga the Hutt's lieutenant. His trunk curls with fury as he levels a heavy blaster at the room. "Nobody steals from the Mighty Varga," he growls, his voice like grinding stone. "Not the girl. Not the droid. And definitely not you."
GM Note — The Fight: Ganga Lor wants to recover the code cylinder and make an example of everyone involved. The fight takes place in cramped quarters — use the environment: toppling droid stacks, shooting steam pipes, activating Switch's security turrets. This is a significant combat. Ganga Lor is killed in this fight. This is a mandatory outcome. He does not escape or retreat. His death establishes that the heroes' actions have real consequences and removes Varga's local enforcer from the board.

Environmental Hazards

Collapsing Droid Stacks

Trigger: Athletics/Physique check or powerful impact.
Effect: Scrap cascades down — creates difficult terrain, pins combatants. At Masterful tier: buries two enforcers, [Restrained] until they dig out. At Legendary: massive collapse blocks the corridor, cuts off reinforcements entirely.
Mitigation: Stay clear of stacks or use them as deliberate area denial.

Steam Pipes

Trigger: Ranged/Reflex check — shooting or striking exposed pipes.
Effect: Superheated steam in a line — burn damage, concealment. At Masterful: full concealment and [Hazard 2] zone, forces enforcers back. At Legendary: multiple pipes rupture, entire corridor becomes a scalding killzone — [Hazard 3].
Mitigation: Breath mask reduces damage. Stay low — steam rises.

Switch's Security Turrets

Trigger: Tech/Wits check (Moderate) or convincing Switch.
Effect: Ceiling turrets fire on hostiles. At Masterful: sustained suppressive fire pins two enforcers specifically. At Legendary: precision targeting, one enforcer hit immediately, rest [Suppressed].
Failure: Turrets activate but target indiscriminately — friendly fire risk. Switch controls targeting; specify targets or turrets fire on everyone.

Cramped Quarters

Active throughout: The bunker is tight. 16 combatants in a 15x10m room. Movement is restricted. Ranged attacks at distance are stepped down due to bodies and obstacles in the way. Melee is king in the corridors but the open center is a killing field. The entry ramp is a chokepoint — Ganga Lor's size blocks it when he's standing in it. After he advances, his enforcers pour in behind him.

Tactical Notes — Positioning

GM Note — Starting Positions: When the hatch blows, the PCs and Maya are at the negotiation table (center). Switch is on his command platform (north). Ganga Lor enters from the hatch (south) and advances into the room. His enforcers split — some follow him in, others try to flank through the east and west scrap corridors. Switch's thugs (if present from the deal) take defensive positions near the platform.
GM Note — Cover Values: The negotiation table provides half-cover from the south (entry) but no cover from the flanks. The droid stacks provide three-quarter cover in the corridors. The command platform provides half-cover from below and elevation advantage. Crates scattered around the floor provide light cover. The entry ramp provides no cover — you're silhouetted against the sky.
GM Note — Mandatory Outcome: Ganga Lor dies here. This is not optional. He is Varga's lieutenant and his death cripples Varga's enforcement on Jakku. Play him as dangerous — he should feel like a real threat — but the narrative requires his death. His enforcers flee or surrender after he falls.
