Command Center — Operations Level

The turbolift opens onto a level that smells different from the court above — machine oil, ozone, and the mineral tang of condensation on old pipes. The lighting is flatter here, more functional, stripped of the amber warmth the guest quarters pretend to offer. Banks of equipment line the walls: server racks blinking in coded patterns, pipes sweating in the humid air, junction boxes trailing cables into the ceiling. At the center of the floor, a command console throws blue holographic light across every surface — a wireframe of the fortress rotating slowly above its projector, each level rendered in clean lines, tiny indicator dots pulsing where systems are active. This is where someone sits and watches everything. The chair at the primary station is worn smooth on the armrests. A half-finished cup of caf has gone cold beside the controls. Beyond the console, the blast doors at the south end stand open, and a draft carries the damp smell of whatever lies below.

Tactical Detail

Armory Cage

Reinforced storage behind mesh gating. Crated supplies, racked blaster rifles with power cells stored separately in a locked cabinet. Disassembled blaster on the work table. Gun oil and packing foam. Moss at the floor joints from the humidity.

Dungeon Stair

Heavy security door at the top of stone stairs descending to the detention block. Temperature drops as you step through. Blue strip lighting, worn treads, the smell of wet stone rising from below. Turns once at a landing before continuing down.

Gamorrean Guard Quarters

Piled sleeping furs, gnawed bones, a rack of vibro-axes and clubs. Clay bowl of something old. Tally marks scratched into the south wall. The smell precedes you. No screens, no terminals, nothing that requires reading. One overhead light nobody turned off.

Guard Weapons Rack

Durasteel table with blaster carbines racked above it — six slots, one empty. Power cells charged and inserted. Spare packs in a crate below. Cleaning rod on the surface. The one bright light on this side of the floor.

Monitoring Bank

Wall-mounted screens in two rows showing live dungeon feeds — corridor sections, the common cell, the central junction, the deeper chamber. Blue-grey night-vision rendering. Feeds cycle every thirty seconds. Chair bolted to a floor track. Half-eaten ration bar on the console shelf.

Command Console

Three-faced durasteel control station with active holographic projector. Fortress wireframe in blue-white light, indicator dots on every active system. Environmental controls, security overrides, comms routing. Cold caf on the primary face. The chair armrests are worn smooth.

Duty Post

Standing-height counter with datapads, flimsi notes, and a status repeater panel mirroring key console readouts. Shift report in careful handwriting. Comms headset on a wall hook. Thermos and two cups. Stool pushed back — recently vacated.

Blast Door Vestibule

Wide corridor between the operations floor and the guest wing above. Floor tiles give way to bare duracrete at the join. Heavy blast doors stand open on pneumatic stops, old blast scoring on the frame. Amber guide strips run toward the doors.

Drainage Access

A low stone opening behind a rusted iron grate — two bolts corroded through, the panel loose at the bottom. Beyond: a narrow passage sloping down in shallow water and mud, tight enough to require crouching. The smell of deep earth and slow water. Passable. Not pleasant.

Sensory Reference

Sound

The constant hum of the command console's power draw. Server fans whining behind the comms array door. Water gurgling through pipes on the east wall. The click of pressure regulators. A draft whistling faintly through the open blast doors from below.

Light

Flat white glow-panels on the operations floor — functional, not atmospheric. Blue holographic wash from the command console, shifting as the wireframe rotates. Amber emergency strips along the power conduit corridor. Blinking indicator lights on the server racks: amber, red, green. Brighter, colder light in the blast door vestibule.

Smell

Machine oil from the armory cage. Ozone from the power conduits. The mineral tang of condensation on bare pipes. Static charge in the comms array. Cold caf and gun-cleaning solvent at the intel desk. Damp, stale air drifting up through the blast doors from the dungeons below.

Temperature

Warmer near the comms array and power conduits. Cooler and more humid near the environmental systems on the east wall. A steady draft from the blast doors keeps the vestibule cooler than the rest of the floor. The command console radiates warmth through the floor plates.

GM Notes — The Operations Level

NARRATIVE FUNCTION: This is Demos's domain — the security hub between the upper court and the dungeons below. When Demos contacts Inquisitor Draco, he does it from the command console. When he monitors the heroes' movements through the fortress, he watches from the monitoring bank. When Darga flees and Demos locks down the lower levels, the controls are here.

TRANSIT POINT: The heroes pass through this level when descending to the dungeons to find Denia, and again when escaping upward through the secret passage. The blast doors at the south end lead to the dungeon stairwell. The upper passage connects to the court level above.

CONFRONTATION SPACE: If the heroes encounter Demos here, the command console provides partial cover (heavy durasteel, chest-height from seated position). The armory cage and comms array are enclosed rooms — defensible positions or traps depending on who gets there first. The power conduits and environmental systems provide flanking corridors on both sides of the main floor.

INTEL OPPORTUNITIES: The monitoring bank can reveal guard positions and patrol routes (Computers/Tech check). The comms array logs may contain Demos's transmissions to the Empire (Slicing check). The intel desk documents — if the heroes take time to read them — reference "the prisoner" and "lower containment."

ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS: The power conduits can be overloaded (Tech check) to kill lighting on this level. The environmental systems valve wheel can flood the floor with water from the pipes — cosmetic hazard, but creates difficult terrain and noise. The blast doors can be sealed from the command console — trapping anyone on the wrong side.
