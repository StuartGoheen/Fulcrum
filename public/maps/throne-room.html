Location Brief — Varga's Throne Room

The audience chamber at the heart of the fortress — where Varga held court, received delegations, and dispensed justice (such as it was). Now empty. The Hutt fled hours ago, his hover-sled carrying him to the hangar and away. The throne still holds the impression of his body. The court is gone. The klaxons are screaming. And standing between you and the exit is the one person who didn't run.

The Setting

Klaxons scream through the stone corridors. The fortress goes into lockdown — blast doors grinding shut, red emergency lighting flooding every hall. Blocking your path stands Igren Demos, the Neimoidian Majordomo. He is no longer simpering. He holds a vibro-staff with lethal confidence, flanked by his elite Trandoshan bodyguards. His red eyes widen as he senses the Jedi behind you. "The Jedi... she's awake. Give her to me. I will take her to the Inquisitorius myself and claim what Varga never could."

The Arena

Audience Chamber

The main floor — wide, open, cracked stone tiles. Room to fight, room to maneuver. Dropped objects from the evacuation. Support columns at the edges provide partial cover. The acoustics carry every sound. Red lockdown lighting pulses from the baseboards.

Varga's Dais

Elevated platform, three steps up. The crimson throne sits empty. Control interfaces in the armrests — all dark. The dais provides high ground and the throne itself is heavy cover. Varga's musk lingers in the fabric.

Secret Passage

The heroes' entry point — steep stairs from the dungeons rising through a concealed floor hatch. Narrow, no railing, barely room for two abreast. The choke point where the fight begins if Demos is waiting for them.

South Vestibule

The bottleneck between the open chamber and the escape route. Two steps down from the main floor — anyone above has the high ground. Demos chose his position here deliberately. Faster lockdown pulse lighting. Drafts from below.

Grand Doors

The main entrance — sealed by lockdown. Massive ornate doors with hydraulic assists, currently locked. Stairs descend into the chamber. The exit the heroes need, if they can get through Demos and open the blast doors.

West Gallery

Narrow gallery along the west wall. Shelving with displayed weapons, a cold brazier, cracked stone. Room for one person — a flanking route, but tight. The displayed weapons are decorative but functional in desperation.

East Alcove

Behind the dais — security panels, a maintenance hatch to tunnels below, one working security feed showing the hangar. A flanking route around the dais, or a secondary escape if the hatch can be opened.

Corner Alcoves

Southwest: deactivated droid, crates, sealed service corridor. Southeast: half-empty weapons rack (stun baton, synthrope), shattered statue. Both provide cover and corners to hide in — or get cornered in.

The Confrontation

GM Note — Demos's Stand: This is the mini-boss fight of Adventure 2. Demos blocks the path between the secret passage and the grand doors — the heroes must get through him to escape. DEMOS'S FORCES: Demos (elite controller, Force-sensitive, vibro-staff) flanked by 2 Trandoshan bodyguards (threats, melee brutes). Demos uses Force Slam to separate heroes emerging from the narrow passage, Force Pull to yank weapons or drag people out of cover. The Trandoshans close on isolated targets. POSITIONING: Demos should be in the south vestibule or the audience chamber floor — controlling the bottleneck. Trandoshans flank wide, one through the west gallery, one toward the east alcove. The heroes emerge from the secret passage into a kill zone. PERSUASION OPTION: A Tier 2 Persuasion check can convince Demos to stand down: "Varga's gone. He left you here. You're fighting for a master who already abandoned you." If successful, Demos hesitates — a moment of bitter clarity — and steps aside. If failed, he fights harder, enraged by the truth. DENIA'S MOMENT: If Denia is with the party and Demos falls, she senses his untrained Force sensitivity. "He was touched by the Force... untrained, but there." This humanizes the kill. If Denia was abandoned in the dungeon, the kill is just a kill. ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS: The brazier in the west gallery can be Force-thrown. The decorative weapons on the wall shelves become projectiles when Demos uses Force Pull. The shattered statue fragments in the southeast are difficult terrain. The maintenance hatch in the east alcove is a secondary escape route — Tech check to open. TIME PRESSURE: Every round spent fighting is a round Varga gains toward escape. Gamorrean reinforcements arrive in 5 rounds if the alarm was already triggered. The heroes should feel the clock.

Sensory Details — Quick Reference

Sound

Klaxons screaming through the stone — a rising, falling wail that echoes off every wall. The hum of sealed blast doors under pressure. Demos's vibro-staff buzzing at combat frequency. The Trandoshans' clawed feet scraping on stone tile. Every footstep, every word, every breath amplified by the chamber's acoustics. The lockdown strips click with each pulse.

Light

Red emergency strips pulsing along every baseboard — rhythmic crimson washes across cracked stone. Amber sconces still burning on the walls, their warm light fighting the red. The dais is a pool of deeper shadow where the crimson throne absorbs the light. Blue indicator panels in the east alcove and southeast corner. The grand doors' lockdown strips glow solid red.

Smell

Varga's cloying musk — still heavy in the air near the throne, soaked into the crimson fabric. Smoke from extinguished braziers. Stale recycled air from emergency ventilation. The metallic tang of Demos's vibro-staff ozone. Sweat from the Trandoshans. From below, the mineral damp of the dungeons drifting up through the open passage.

Temperature

Warm and stale — the environmental systems are on emergency, recycling without refreshing. Cooler near the secret passage where dungeon air rises. The stone walls hold residual warmth from the court session hours ago. Near the grand doors, the sealed blast door radiates cold from the corridor beyond. The dais platform is slightly warmer — Varga's repulsor systems still running on standby, generating ambient heat.

GM Note — After the Fight: Once Demos is defeated or persuaded, the path to the grand doors is clear. The blast door lockdown requires a Tech check or Fusion Cutter to bypass. Beyond the doors: the corridor to the hangar bay (Scene 9). The clock is ticking — Draco's Star Destroyer is inbound, Varga is already running, and Gamorrean reinforcements are converging. The heroes need to move.
