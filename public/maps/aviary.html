Location Brief — The Aviary

A controlled biome chamber deep inside Varga's fortress on Takodana. Originally designed as an agricultural research module — hydroponic growth systems, atmospheric controls, artificial day-night cycling — repurposed by Varga as a private garden and exotic bird collection. The chamber is roughly thirty meters across, octagonal, with angled durasteel walls softened by climbing moss and engineered vegetation. Overhead, full-spectrum grow lights simulate Takodana's forest canopy. The air is warm, humid, and heavy with the smell of soil and green growth. Every surface that isn't durasteel is alive.

The Garden

You step through the blast door and the fortress vanishes. In its place — a jungle. Dense vegetation presses in from every direction, leaves brushing your arms, moisture beading on your skin. Blue-white grow lights hum overhead, casting the garden in an artificial twilight. Somewhere above, a bird calls — bright, sharp, echoing off unseen walls. Tree stumps rise from the undergrowth like ancient sentinels, their carved tops scored with claw marks. A path of flat stones winds through the center of the chamber, the only clear ground in a sea of engineered green. The air smells of wet earth and something else — something animal. Feathers drift in the warm updraft from the western wall. Then you hear it: a low, clicking trill from the canopy above. Not a songbird. Something with talons.

Terrain

The stone path is the only clear ground — full movement speed, no cover. Everything else is dense undergrowth: difficult terrain with concealment but slow, noisy movement. The bioluminescent fungi in the western undergrowth glow brighter when disturbed, betraying movement. The bone pit and the southeast roost have the worst footing — slippery with organic debris. The canopy walk offers elevation but is exposed and creaks under weight.

Elevation

Perch platforms throughout the aviary rise 1-2 meters above the floor — the raptors' natural vantage points. The canopy walk in the north-center connects the tallest platforms with bark bridges. The environmental control station is at floor level but sheltered by an overhang. The grow light banks are wall-mounted, 2-3 meters up — climbable but not designed as platforms.

Environmental Controls

The southwest control station can: kill all lights (darkness, 1 round before emergency backup), trigger fire-suppression mist (full concealment, 2 rounds), activate irrigation flood (ankle-deep mud, difficult terrain everywhere), or lock/unlock doors. The secondary terminal in the feeding station has readouts only. Smashing grow light panels creates local darkness and sparking debris.

The Raptors

Four Takodana Raptors — pack hunters, fast, territorial. They know the terrain intimately. They roost in the southeast, hunt from the perch platforms, and dive from the canopy walk. Kessra directs them with whistle commands from an elevated position. Killing or driving off Kessra disrupts their coordination — they revert to territorial defense of the southeast roost rather than coordinated pack tactics.

The Ambush

The first raptor hits before you see it — a blur of brown and gold dropping from the canopy, talons extended, a shriek that splits the air. The second comes from the undergrowth to your left, low and fast, using the vegetation as cover. Somewhere above and to the east, a sharp whistle — two notes, descending — and the raptors adjust their angle of attack in unison. Someone is directing them. Through the leaves, on the highest perch platform in the northeast: a Zygerrian woman in dark leather, a control whistle between her teeth, watching you with undisguised hatred. Kessra. She followed you into the fortress, and she's been waiting for this.
GM Note — The Aviary Ambush: This is a conditional encounter — it only triggers if the heroes humiliated Kessra during the Blackwind Point landing scene. Kessra has infiltrated the fortress (she has contacts among Varga's security staff), released the raptors from their roost, and locked the aviary doors behind her. She commands from the canopy walk or northeast tech bay, using whistle commands to direct the raptors. TACTICAL PRIORITIES: The raptors are the immediate threat, but Kessra is the force multiplier. While she commands, the raptors coordinate — flanking, isolating, driving prey toward each other. If Kessra is taken down or driven off, the raptors break coordination and retreat to the southeast roost to defend their territory. The environmental control station is the heroes' best tactical asset — darkness, mist, or flooding all disrupt the raptors' hunting patterns. ESCAPE: The aviary doors are locked. Options: reach the control station and unlock remotely, pick the lock at the southeast entrance, or force the door (noisy, attracts fortress guards). Kessra does not want to be caught by Varga's people — if the fight turns against her, she flees through the maintenance crawlspace behind the east grow lights. The encounter rewards tactical thinking over raw combat power. The raptors are minions — individually weak but dangerous in a pack in enclosed terrain. The real challenge is the environment.

Sensory Details — Quick Reference

Smell

Wet soil and decomposing leaves — a forest floor compressed into an enclosed space. The sweetness of engineered flowers mixed with the sharp musk of bird droppings. Near the grow lights, the ozone tang of electrical discharge. Near the southeast roost, the unmistakable copper-and-iron smell of a predator's den — old kills, bone fragments, dried blood on wood.

Sound

The constant hum of the grow lights — a low electrical drone that fades into background. Bird calls: songbirds chattering, the occasional piercing alarm shriek of a sunwing. The drip of irrigation water. Leaves rustling in the artificial updrafts. And beneath it all, when the raptors are hunting: silence. The songbirds go quiet when the predators move. That silence is the warning.

Temperature

Warm and humid — the atmospheric controls maintain Takodana forest conditions. The air is thick enough to taste. The western wall radiates heat from the grow lights. The control station corner is cooler — ventilation ducts pull excess moisture. The canopy walk catches the warm updraft, making it the warmest spot in the chamber. Sweat beads on every surface, organic and inorganic.

Light

Blue-white grow lights create an artificial twilight — bright enough to see but with deep shadows in the undergrowth. The light cycles irregularly on the east side where panels are damaged, creating a strobe effect. The control station glows blue from its holographic displays. The bioluminescent fungi pulse faintly in the western undergrowth. The canopy walk is the best-lit area — exposed under the overhead lights. The southeast roost is the darkest — the vegetation is thickest and the lights don't fully penetrate.

GM Note — Combat Spacing: The aviary is designed as a predator's hunting ground, not a fair fight. The stone path is the kill zone — fast movement but total exposure. The undergrowth offers concealment but slows movement and alerts the bioluminescent fungi. The canopy walk gives elevation advantage but makes you a target from every angle. The control station is the tactical objective — reaching it gives the heroes control of light, mist, and door locks, but it's in the far corner from the entrance. The southeast roost is the raptors' stronghold and the location of the exit door. The feeding station has improvised weapons. The grow light banks can be weaponized for local darkness or electrical hazards. Kessra commands from elevation — getting line of sight on her through the vegetation is the first challenge. Everything in this encounter is about terrain control: who can see whom, who can move freely, and who controls the environment.
