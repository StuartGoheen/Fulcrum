Location Brief — Guest Quarters (Knife in the Dark)

The guest wing of Varga's fortress on Takodana — five rooms arranged along a central corridor, with a shared refresher, a small common area, and a south foyer connecting to the fortress interior. The rooms are functional, not luxurious — better than a bunk on a freighter, worse than a mid-range hotel on Corellia. The fortress provides them to visiting delegations, business contacts, and anyone Varga wants close enough to watch. The heroes share Room 5, the lower west room at the far end of the wing — farthest from the turbolift, deepest into the corridor. It feels safe. That's the problem.

The Setting

You wake to the sound of something wrong. Not a noise — the absence of noise. The fortress hum is still there, the distant rumble of generators and the drip of jungle condensation. But the corridor outside your quarters has gone silent. No guard footsteps. No droid patrol. Then: a shadow moves at the edge of your door. Too low for a guard. Too fast for a servant. The lock clicks. Not forced — opened with a code. Three figures slide into the room. The first is massive — a Wookiee, moving with terrifying silence for something that size. Behind him, lean and quiet, is Skreev. And behind both of them, face hidden in shadow, is Narek. They're here to kill you.

The Quarters

Room Layout

Five guest rooms — three along the west wall, two on the east — flanking a central corridor. Each room has two beds, a refresher alcove, and a single arched door with an electronic lock. A shared refresher sits opposite the lower west room. The rooms are small — close quarters, limited sightlines, tight spaces between the beds and walls. At night, the glow-panels dim to their lowest setting, and the blue baseboard strips are the only light. The heroes share Room 5, the lower west room.

The Corridor

Rust-brown carpet over stone. Absorbs footsteps. Wide enough for two abreast but not comfortably. Arched doorways on both sides, warm amber sconces between them. At night, the corridor is a tunnel of dim light and deep shadow — you can see from end to end, but you can't see into the doorways until you're standing in them.

South Foyer & Common Area

The corridor opens south into a wider space with polished stone flooring, a holographic display terminal cycling a fortress map, and a small common lounge with benches and a holoscreen. Higher ceiling, different acoustics. Two exits: south into the fortress interior, southeast into a service corridor. A comm terminal alcove sits against the west wall.

Service Access

The service corridor in the southeast leads to the fortress's working levels — lower ceiling, bare durasteel, blue-white floor guides. This is how staff and maintenance droids move through the building. A utility alcove near the junction holds environmental controls and a droid charging station.

The Ambush

GM Note — Knife in the Dark: This is a nighttime assassination attempt in the heroes' quarters. The attackers are Grakkus (Wookiee enforcer — close combat monster in confined space), Skreev (Quarren assassin — ranged, hangs back), and conditionally Narek (Twi'lek poisoner — only present if the heroes exposed or humiliated him during the poison subplot; otherwise replace with an Iron Ring enforcer). The assassins enter through the main corridor using a stolen door code. They pass four empty rooms before reaching Room 5 at the far end of the west wall. They have surprise unless a hero succeeds on an Awareness check (the absence of guard patrols is the tell). Heroes are unarmored. Weapons may not be accessible without a retroactive Skulduggery check ("I always sleep with my blaster under the pillow"). TACTICAL ENVIRONMENT: Room 5 is the kill zone — confined, one door, the Wookiee's reach filling the space. Only two combatants can engage in melee simultaneously in the doorway. The corridor offers more space but the assassins control it — the heroes must fight through them to reach the foyer. The south foyer gives room to maneuver and access to the common area, comm terminal, and service corridor. The service corridor is an escape route — or a flanking path if the assassins are clever. THE HEROES' ROOM: The party shares Room 5. Their gear is stacked against the south wall. Bedrolls on the floor between the beds. The sabacc game on the table. The door lock was set — but the assassins have the code. The scene's purpose is consequence — the court's dangers catching up. It breaks the investigation rhythm and reminds the heroes that Varga's court kills.

Sensory Details — Quick Reference

Sound

The fortress never stops humming — generators, environmental systems, the low throb of power running through stone walls. At night, the corridor is silent except for the periodic creak of settling architecture and the drip of condensation from the jungle outside. The carpet eats footsteps. The electronic door locks click when they cycle — a small, precise sound that carries in the silence.

Light

Glow-panels dim to low amber at night. Blue baseboard strips provide navigation-level light — enough to see shapes, not faces. The holographic display in the south foyer casts a cold blue glow that pulses as the map rotates. The service corridor has floor-level guide lights that throw shadows upward.

Smell

Chemical sanitizer from the refresher alcoves. The faint ozone of electronic systems. Moisture in the lower rooms from the fortress drainage. The rust-brown carpet smells of dust and old fabric softener. In the service corridor, the air is drier and carries the tang of heavy electrical equipment.

Temperature

The upper rooms are temperate — climate-controlled, comfortable. The lower rooms are warmer and more humid, closer to the fortress infrastructure. The service corridor is cooler and drier. The stone walls radiate a chill that seeps through bare feet on tile. The carpet in the corridor is warmer underfoot — a small mercy in the dark.

GM Note — Combat Spacing: Room 5 is the kill zone — confined, one exit, the Wookiee's reach filling the space between the beds. The corridor is no-man's-land — more room to fight but the assassins control it from the approach. The four empty rooms (1-4) offer cover and flanking — a hero who breaks past the assassins could duck into an empty room and ambush from a doorway. The shared refresher is a hiding spot or a dead end — one door, slippery tile floor. The south foyer is the escape valve — room to maneuver, access to the common area, and the comm terminal if someone needs to call for help (though calling fortress security means explaining why assassins had your door code). The service corridor is the back door — an escape route toward the fortress working levels. If the heroes break out of Room 5, they need to decide: fight through the corridor, barricade in an empty room, or sprint for the foyer. Every choice has a cost.
