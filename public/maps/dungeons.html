Location Brief — The Dungeons

The restricted lower levels beneath Varga's fortress — an old Neimoidian detention block repurposed by the Hutt for high-value prisoners. The block is cold, damp, and humming with machinery. Ancient inscriptions cover every stone surface, remnants of the building's original purpose. Somewhere above, the fortress operates as normal. Down here, it's silent except for the force cage systems and the containment fields. Two prisoners the heroes know about — and others they don't.

The Setting

TC-663's intercepted comms mentioned "the prisoner" — someone hidden in the dungeon that Demos wants to sell to the Inquisitorius. You descend into the restricted lower levels, past sealed blast doors and silent guard droids. The detention block is cold and humming with machinery. In the deepest chamber, a force cage rises from the floor — shimmering energy, a narrow bed inside, a human woman in tattered Jedi robes lying perfectly still. Medical equipment surrounds the cage, monitors pulsing. She is breathing. Barely. Her eyes are closed. But you feel something — a whisper at the edge of your consciousness. Not words. A feeling. Like drowning in slow motion. In the barred common cell above, Warrick Raden sits against the wall. Beaten, bruised, but alive. He looks up with hollow eyes.

The Block

Common Cell

The large barred cell — iron grating, cold stone, no technology. Warrick Raden is inside. Beaten and bruised, but alive. Tray of untouched food near the bars. No containment field, no electronics — just old iron and stone and the smell of blood.

Ascending Passage

An ornate archway with glowing Neimoidian inscriptions marks the entrance to a steep, narrow passage climbing upward — single file only. The only exit that doesn't lead back through the guard room. The passage emerges into the throne room level above.

West Holding Cell

Containment cell on the west side of the central junction. Blue field, bare stone, scratch marks on the back wall. Occupied — a figure sits inside watching the corridor. Biometric lock panel on the outside wall.

Central Junction

The widest point in the block — vaulted ceiling, elaborate inscribed floor. Two real-flame torches provide the only warm light in the dungeon. The one space with room to move. Containment cells visible to the west and east.

East Holding Cell

Empty containment cell on the east side of the junction. Field left active, holding nothing. Clean floor — no signs of recent use.

Denia's Containment Area

The largest chamber in the lower dungeon. A force cage holds a narrow bed. Jedi Master Denia lies inside — barely breathing, eyes closed, medical equipment keeping her in a coma. The air is cold with preservatives. Something reaches outward from this room that has no name.

East Cells

Two stacked containment cells along the east wall of the lower dungeon. One holds an unknown prisoner curled on the floor, unmoving. The other is empty and dark, strip light burned out. Both fields active.

Dungeon Chamber

The main inscribed floor connecting the lower cells. Deeply carved Neimoidian geometric patterns and ancient script cover every stone surface. Blue strip lighting at ankle height. Cold, still air. Old in a way the upper levels aren't.

Discovery & Decision

GM Note — Discovering Denia: This is the campaign's first major pivot. Two discoveries, one decision. JEDI MASTER DENIA: Found in stasis since shortly after Order 66. Kept by Varga as a trophy to auction to the Inquisitorius. She's weak, barely alive, and telepathically reaching out. Varth advises abandonment: "Leave her. She's a liability. We don't have time for dead weight." The heroes decide. WARRICK RADEN: Found beaten in the east cell. The heroes rescued him from the hit squad, he got them inside, and Varga threw him in the dungeon. He's alive and can walk, but he's broken. VARTH'S RED FLAG: His voice doesn't change. That's what makes it chilling. He sounds the same as always — pragmatic, efficient, reasonable. "Leave her. She's a liability." He's not being cruel. He's being operational. And that's worse. DEMOS/DRACO HOLO-CALL: While in the dungeon, the heroes can overhear (or TC-663 alerts them to) a holo-call in the comms alcove between Demos and Inquisitor Draco. Demos reports the prisoner is viable. Draco's response: "I am on my way, with the Assiduous." The death clock starts — Draco's Star Destroyer is inbound.

Sensory Details — Quick Reference

Sound

The constant hum of the stasis machinery — low, resonant, felt in the teeth and chest. Containment fields buzzing at different pitches. Water moving somewhere beneath the floor grates. The slow pulse of subterranean machinery. Silence between sounds — a dead, heavy silence that presses on the ears.

Light

Blue strip lighting at ankle height along every wall, casting upward shadows. The blue-green glow of stasis equipment through frosted viewport glass. Pale blue shimmer of containment fields. Two real-flame torches at the central junction — the only warm light in the block. The guard station monitor casts grey-blue across the desk. Everything else is shadow.

Smell

Cold stone and mineral dust. Medical preservatives from the stasis cell — sharp, antiseptic. Damp — condensation on stone walls, moisture seeping from somewhere below. Rust from old fixtures. The faint ozone of containment fields. In Raden's cell: blood and fear.

Temperature

Cold. The stasis cell radiates refrigerated air through the grating. The stone walls are cold to the touch — deep underground cold, not climate-controlled cold. Slightly warmer near the central junction torches and in the guard station. The containment fields generate a subtle warmth at close range. Your breath doesn't quite fog, but it's close.

GM Note — Tactical Environment: The stasis cell requires a Tech check to open safely — fumble and Denia dies. The containment fields require Skulduggery or a biometric bypass (Tech) to drop. The guard station turbolift is locked but leads to the operations level above — an escape route if they can crack the code. The central junction is the only space with room to fight — the corridors and cells are too narrow for more than two combatants abreast. The comms alcove is sound-dampened — good for eavesdropping on Demos, but a dead end if cornered. If the alarm triggers, elite guard droids activate — heavy armor, vulnerable to ion damage. FORCE ECHO: If Denia is disturbed from stasis, every Force-sensitive character in the block feels nausea and dread. Igren Demos senses the disturbance from anywhere in the palace. This is unavoidable — the price of rescue.
