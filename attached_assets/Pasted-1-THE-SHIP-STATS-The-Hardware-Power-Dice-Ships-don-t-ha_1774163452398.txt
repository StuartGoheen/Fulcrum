1. THE SHIP STATS (The Hardware / Power Dice)
Ships don't have Arenas; they have 5 Hardware Systems that range from a d4 to a d12. These provide the Power Die for the crew's actions.

Handling: Agility and turn rate. (Used for dodging).

Shields: Deflector grid and energy absorption. (Used to endure hits).

Engines: Raw linear speed and hyperdrive. (Used for movement/escapes).

Weapons: Heavy cannons and torpedoes. (Used for dealing damage).

Sensors: Scanners, targeting, and comms. (Used for slicing and electronic warfare).

(Just like characters, ships have a Vitality/Integrity track, and players can spend Marks/Credits to "Step Up" their ship's hardware dice).

2. THE CREW STATIONS (The Meatware / Control Dice)
Players use their Disciplines (Control Die) combined with the ship's Hardware (Power Die) to act.

The Pilot: Rolls Piloting + Handling to dodge attacks or cross Range Zones (Engaged, Close, Far).

The Engineer: Rolls Tech + Shields to absorb heavy hits, or Tech + Engines to overclock systems and clear Strain.

The Gunner: Rolls Ranged + Weapons to deal massive damage.

The Operator: Rolls Sense/Tech + Sensors to slice enemy ships, inflict conditions like [Winded], or paint targets.

The Commander: Rolls Presence + Sensors to buff the crew, grant [Optimized] conditions, or fast-talk enemy comms.

3. PLAYER-FACING DEFENSE & OPENINGS
Because your system uses player-facing rolls, the GM never rolls for the Imperial Cruiser; the players roll to survive it.

Evasive Maneuvers (Dodge): The Pilot rolls to physically dodge the blast.

Angle Deflectors (Endure): The Engineer rolls to absorb the blast with the shields.

The Payoff: If either the Pilot or Engineer rolls a Mastery (8+) on their Control die, they perfectly outplay the enemy, creating a physical [Opening].

The Economy: The Gunner can immediately spend a Trigger to exploit that Opening, firing the ship's weapons out of turn!

4. THE RULE OF SCALE
There are 5 Scales: Character (1) > Vehicle (2) > Starship (3) > Capital Ship (4) > Space Station (5).
When two entities of different scales interact, you subtract the difference and apply that many "Steps" to the player's dice pool:

When the Target is LARGER than the Player: Step UP your Control Die (they are massive, easy to see/hit), but Step DOWN your Power Die (your tiny lasers struggle to dent their heavy armor).

When the Target is SMALLER than the Player: Step DOWN your Control Die (it is incredibly hard to track/hit a buzzing mosquito), but Step UP your Power Die (if your massive turbolaser connects, they are instantly vaporized).

This makes an X-Wing (Scale 3) fighting a Star Destroyer (Scale 4) mechanically terrifying without requiring the GM to use massive multiplier tables.
