Adventure 1: The Traitors Gambit
Part 1 The Jakku Job
Setting the Scene: Niima Outpost The campaign opens not in the sterile corridors of a Core World space station, but in the sweltering, wind-scoured scrap settlement of Niima Outpost on Jakku. It is 16 BBY, the era of the "Young Empire." Here, Imperial law is a distant rumor; the only authority is the person with the heaviest blaster or the most water. The aesthetic is "scavenger chic"—tarps flapping in the wind, structures built from the rusted ribs of fallen starships, and a populace covered in dust and grease.
The heroes are gathered in Sienar’s Regret, a cantina constructed inside the hollowed-out, sand-blasted fuselage of a crashed separatist transport. The lighting is dim, provided by failing glow-rods, and the air is thick with the smell of cheap spice, sweat, and ozone. The heroes are here for their own reasons—hiding out, looking for work, or gambling away their last credits.
Scene 1: Bad Business at Sienar's Regret (In Media Res) The adventure begins immediately with action. The blast doors of the cantina hiss open violently, admitting the blinding white glare of the Jakku sun. A young human woman, Maya, bursts in. She is disheveled, clutching a datapad to her chest, and clearly terrified. She isn't running from Stormtroopers; she is fleeing the local enforcers of the rising crime lord, Darga the Hutt.
Maya scans the room, desperation in her eyes. She spots the player characters—perhaps they are the only ones armed, or simply the only ones who don't look like they've given up on life. She sprints toward their table. As she reaches them, she intentionally hooks her foot on a chair leg and crashes into their table, sending drinks and credits scattering. It creates a chaotic scene, drawing every eye in the room.
Before the heroes can react, her pursuers enter. A heavy-set Weequay and two Kyuzo enforcers (wearing wide-brimmed war helms) storm in, blasters drawn. They see Maya cowering behind the heroes and make a fatal assumption: the heroes are her muscle. The Weequay barks a threat in Huttese, raising a heavy vibro-ax.
This is the campaign's inciting incident. The thugs are shooting to kill, forcing the heroes to defend themselves. This replaces the "Promenade Shootout" from the source material. The heroes aren't fighting for the Rebellion; they are fighting because a grifter dragged them into a bar brawl.
The Pitch: Once the smoke clears and the thugs are dealt with, the dust settles. Maya, catching her breath, doesn’t offer a noble speech about democracy. She offers a business proposition. She admits she used the heroes as a shield, but claims it was necessary to protect "The Key." She reveals she has stolen an encrypted Imperial Code Cylinder from Darga’s operation. She claims it leads to the "score of a lifetime"—the location of a stash of credits embezzled by a former Imperial Admiral named Gilder Varth. She needs the heroes to help her decrypt it and retrieve the map. In exchange, she offers them a cut of the fortune.
Scene 2: The Processor Maya explains that she cannot crack the Imperial encryption herself. She leads the heroes out of the outpost and into the Starship Graveyard to meet her contact. They navigate through the ribs of fallen capital ships and dunes of rust until they reach "The Processor," a subterranean bunker built from buried shipping containers.
This is the domain of Switch, a protocol droid who has forgone memory wipes for decades to become a crime lord and information broker. Unlike the pristine station in the source material, Switch’s lair is a technological cave. Wires hang from the ceiling like vines, and the floor is a carpet of droid parts. However, in the center of the filth sits a pristine, priceless desk of Japor ivory wood—a visual cue of Switch’s influence and wealth.
Switch agrees to decrypt the cylinder for a cut of the profit. As he works, the GM should emphasize the "Western" tension—Switch is not an ally, he is a businessman holding all the cards. As the decryption finishes, he reveals a twist: the cylinder doesn't contain coordinates to a vault. It contains a shipping manifest for a "biological asset" currently held in a local warlord's hangar.
Scene 3: The Gangster’s Revenge Just as Switch reveals the manifest, the bunker’s motion sensors scream. Ganga Lor, Darga the Hutt’s lieutenant, has tracked them down. In the original adventure, Ganga Lor is a Chevin; this species works perfectly here—a massive, trunked alien that looks monstrous in the flickering lights of the bunker.
Ganga Lor blows the hatch. He doesn't just want the cylinder; he wants to make an example of everyone involved to maintain Darga’s reputation. The fight takes place in the cramped confines of the bunker. The heroes must use the environment—toppling stacks of droid parts, shooting steam pipes, or using Switch’s personal security turrets—to survive.
The Intel: Upon defeating Ganga Lor, Switch finishes his analysis. He projects a hologram, not of credits, but of a person: The Accountant. This man, an Imperial logistics officer, managed the embezzled funds for Admiral Varth. Switch reveals that The Accountant was captured by a local proxy warlord. To ensure he didn't talk before he could be sold back to the Empire, he was frozen in carbonite.
The "score" is currently sitting in a holding bay at the Niima Landing Field (replacing "Blue Deck"), waiting for an Imperial shuttle to pick it up. If the heroes want the location of the money, they have to steal the carbonite block now.
Scene 4: The Heist The narrative moves to the Niima Landing Field. This replaces the "Blue Deck" from the source material. Instead of a luxury docking bay, this is a militarized zone of the outpost controlled by the warlord.
The heroes must infiltrate the hangar. This is their first brush with the "Young Empire." While the warlord’s thugs guard the perimeter, inside the hangar the heroes see their first true sign of Imperial occupation: a squad of Stormtroopers (perhaps dirty and wearing transition-era armor) overseeing the transfer. An Imperial shuttle is on final approach. The clock is ticking.
The heroes have options: bluff their way past the warlord's guards as the Imperial pickup crew, sneak through maintenance vents, or launch a direct assault using the heavy machinery in the hangar as cover.
The Climax of 1A: The heroes secure the carbonite block containing The Accountant. As they load it onto their transport (or a stolen skiff), the Imperial shuttle lands, and they must flee under fire. This provides a high-octane exit from Jakku.
The Cliffhanger: Once safe in hyperspace or deep in the desert, the heroes thaw the carbonite block. The Accountant coughs to life, disoriented and terrified. When the heroes demand the location of the credits, he laughs bitterly. He reveals that Admiral Varth is the only one with the codes to the account.
The Accountant drops the bombshell: Varth isn't dead or hiding in luxury. He is being held at a secret prison facility on the jungle moon of Ajan Kloss in the Cademimu Sector. The warlord holding him is about to trade him to the Empire. If the heroes want their payday, they have to break Varth out of prison before the Empire takes custody


Part 2: The Green Hell Extraction
The Setup: The Blockade Having escaped Jakku with The Accountant (or the data he possessed), the heroes know that Admiral Gilder Varth is being held by a proxy warlord, General Skarn, on the jungle moon of Ajan Kloss in the Cademimu Sector. The Accountant warns that the Empire is aware of Varth's location and has established a blockade around the sector to secure him. The heroes cannot simply fly in; they need a route that bypasses the Imperial Venator-class Star Destroyers patrolling the hyperlanes.
Scene 1: The Banshee To navigate the blockade, the heroes' contact (Maya or Switch) directs them to rendezvous with Captain Sirona Okeefe and her modified Baudo-class star yacht, the Banshee. Okeefe is a veteran smuggler operating on the fringes of the Western Reaches.
The Meeting: The heroes meet Okeefe in deep space. She is impressed they have their own ship but warns that the "Hyperspace Sinkholes" and Imperial patrols make a standard approach suicide. She offers to upload a proprietary "smuggler’s vector" to their nav-computer—a dangerous route through debris fields—in exchange for a cut of the Varth bounty.
The Run: The heroes must pilot their ship following Okeefe’s telemetry. Upon reverting to realspace above Ajan Kloss, they immediately detect a Victory-class Star Destroyer and a wing of V-19 Torrents or V-wings patrolling the orbit.
Scene 2: The Crash To evade the planetary sensor net established by General Skarn, the heroes must perform a high-speed, "nap-of-the-earth" atmospheric entry.
The Descent: As they break the cloud layer, the lush, overgrown jungle of Ajan Kloss rushes up to meet them. The moon is a "Green Hell," dominated by massive, ancient trees and fungal overgrowth.
The Impact: During the descent, a glancing hit from a perimeter defense turret or a collision with the massive canopy forces a hard landing. The ship shears through the treeline and skids to a halt in the mud. The ship is safe from sensors but grounded until repairs are made. The heroes are miles from Skarn’s fortress, the "Vanishing Place," and must proceed on foot.
Scene 3: The Jungle Trek The heroes trek through the humid, hostile wilds of Ajan Kloss. The environment is lethal; the flora is semi-sentient, and the fauna is hungry.
Hazards: The heroes must navigate Acid Pools disguised as standing water and avoid Exploding Fungus Blooms that detonate when disturbed.
The Guide: They are ambushed not by Imperials, but by Vazus Mandrake. In this reskin, Mandrake is a weary Separatist Holdout Commando who refused the shut-down order three years ago and has been waging a one-man guerilla war against Warlord Skarn.
The Deal: Mandrake holds the heroes at gunpoint, assuming they are Imperial scouts. Once convinced otherwise (likely by their mutual enemy), he agrees to guide them to a secret drainage sluice entering the fortress. In exchange, they must agree to help him sabotage the facility's generator to aid his guerilla war.
Scene 4: The Vanishing Place (Infiltration) Mandrake leads the heroes to the facility. It is not a pristine Imperial garrison, but a repurposed, moss-covered temple ruin fortified with scavenged Clone Wars technology.
The Observation: From a ridge, the heroes see the situation is critical. An Imperial Lambda-class Shuttle sits on the landing pad. A squad of pristine Stormtroopers stands at attention, a stark contrast to Skarn’s grimy mercenaries.
The Intel: Inside the perimeter, the heroes infiltrate the Medical Bay, where Captain Vischera (an Imperial scientist on loan to Skarn) conducts biological experiments on local wildlife. In the Communications Center, they intercept a transmission confirming that the Imperial officer outside is stalling. Inquisitor Valin Draco is en route to take personal custody of Varth. This puts a hard clock on the mission: they must grab Varth before the Inquisitor lands.
Scene 5: The Detention Block The heroes fight their way to the detention block, dealing with Skarn’s elite guards and automated defenses.
The Twist: They blow the door to the holding cell expecting to find a broken man. Instead, they find Admiral Gilder Varth sitting comfortably, perhaps gambling with a confused guard or packing a bag. He is arrogant, charming, and unbothered.
The Interaction: Varth treats the rescue like a hired service. "You're late. I had credits on the second moonrise." He refuses to leave without his encrypted data cylinders, which he claims contain the ledger for Project Leviathan. He reveals that he didn't defect for moral reasons; he embezzled a fortune from the project and needs the heroes to get him—and his money—out.
Scene 6: The Deal Gone Bad (Climax) As the heroes drag Varth toward the exit, the deal on the landing pad collapses. The Empire tires of Skarn’s negotiation.
The Chaos: The Stormtroopers open fire on Skarn’s mercenaries. The fortress erupts into a three-way firefight: Imperials vs. Warlord vs. Heroes.
The Shadow: As the heroes sprint across the courtyard or hangar bay, a shadow falls over them. Draco’s shuttle screams overhead. They barely make it into the dense jungle canopy as the Inquisitor lands, deploying Shadow Troopers to cut off their retreat.
The Extraction: Captain Okeefe swoops in with the Banshee (or the heroes' repaired ship), strafing the Imperial positions to buy the heroes time to board. They blast off just as Imperial walkers breach the treeline.
The Conclusion: Safe in hyperspace, Varth reveals the truth: Project Leviathan is a massive mobile siege platform being built in the Unknown Regions. He skimmed millions of credits from the budget, and the codes to access that fortune are held by his former partner, Darga the Hutt. This sets the stage for the next leg of the campaign: a meeting with a Hutt, not to save the galaxy, but to rob a bank.
Admiral Gilder Varth: The Man Who Sold the Galaxy
Archetype: The High-Stakes Grifter / The Corrupt Quartermaster Visuals: A man in his late 50s who wears an Imperial Admiral’s uniform like a lounging robe—top button undone, sleeves rolled up, cap usually resting on the table beside a drink. He has a winning smile, tired but intelligent eyes, and he is constantly manipulating a platinum credit chip or a deck of sabacc cards in his hands.
I. Biography: The Art of the Steal
The Logistics Genius Gilder Varth was never a frontline warrior. While other officers studied orbital bombardment tactics, Varth studied supply chains. He rose through the ranks of the Imperial Navy not by winning battles, but by making sure the Grand Moffs got their caviar and the Star Destroyers got their tibanna gas. He realized early on that the Empire was so massive, and its bureaucracy so bloated, that millions of credits could vanish into the rounding errors without anyone noticing.
Project Leviathan His masterpiece was his assignment to Project Leviathan in the Deep Core/Unknown Regions. As the Chief Logistics Officer for this massive mobile siege platform, Varth oversaw a budget large enough to buy entire star systems. He didn't just skim off the top; he diverted entire convoys of raw materials—Doonium, Coaxium, and slave labor—into private accounts and hidden caches in the Western Reaches.
The Exit Strategy Around 16 BBY, the Imperial Security Bureau (ISB) began an audit. Varth knew his time was up. He didn't defect to the Rebellion because of a moral awakening; he ran because he wanted to spend his stolen fortune. He fled to the Western Reaches, intending to disappear into the lawless frontier. He partnered with the crime lord Darga the Hutt to launder his assets.
The Double-Cross Varth’s plan hit a snag. His "partner," Darga, froze the accounts, and his "protector," Warlord Skarn, realized that Varth was worth more as a prisoner than a guest. Skarn threw Varth into the detention block on Ajan Kloss, intending to sell him back to Inquisitor Draco. This is where the heroes find him: broke, cornered, but already planning his next hand.

II. Personality: "Trust No One (Especially Me)"
The "Beckett" Vibe Varth is cool under pressure because he assumes everyone is corruptible. He treats the heroes not as saviors, but as talented freelancers he has hired for a job. He is charming, cynical, and brutally honest about the nature of the galaxy, even if he is lying about his own intentions.
Cynical Wisdom: He mentors the heroes, but his lessons are about survival, not morality. "The Empire, the Republic, the Separatists—it’s just different logos on the same blaster. The only thing that matters is who is holding it."
The Gambler: He frames military operations as heists or wagers. He doesn't say, "We need to infiltrate the base"; he says, "The house has the odds, so we need to cheat."
Unflappable: When the heroes rescue him from Ajan Kloss, he isn't cowering. He’s annoyed they are late. "I had a bet with the guard that I’d be out by sunset. You cost me fifty credits."
Motivation: The Long Con Varth is motivated by Self-Preservation and Greed, in that order.
Phase 1 (The Heroes): He uses the heroes to escape execution by the Inquisitorius.
Phase 2 (The Hutt): He uses the heroes to eliminate Darga the Hutt. He claims this is to "hurt the Empire's supply lines," but in reality, he needs Darga dead to regain access to his stolen fortune.
Phase 3 (The Buy-In): He realizes he cannot run forever. He plans to take his stolen data and the "secured" supply chain back to the Emperor. He will claim his "defection" was a deep-cover operation to root out corruption (Darga) and Rebels (The Heroes). He plans to buy his way back in as a Grand Admiral.

III. Roleplaying Varth at the Table
Mannerisms & Tics
The Coin: He constantly rolls a credit coin over his knuckles. When he makes a decision, he flips it.
The Datapad: He is glued to his datapad, checking "investments" even during firefights.
Nicknames: He rarely uses the heroes' real names. He calls them "Kid," "Pilot," "Heavy," or "Jedi" (said with a mock whisper).
Key Interactions
With the Empire: He holds his former colleagues in contempt, viewing them as stiff, unimaginative bureaucrats. "Look at them. They march in straight lines. That's why they're so easy to trip."
With the Heroes: He acts like the "Cool Uncle." He compliments their violence. "Nice shot, kid. Remind me never to owe you money." This builds rapport, making his eventual betrayal in Adventure 10 devastating.

IV. The Narrative Arc (GM Secrets)
Adventure 1 (Ajan Kloss): Varth is the "Damsel in Distress." He plays up the "disillusioned defector" angle to get the heroes to trust him, but drops hints that he knows where the money is.
Adventure 2 (Cato Neimoidia): Varth is the "Mission Control." He sends the heroes to Darga’s palace. He emphasizes that Darga is "funding the Empire." This is true, but Varth just wants Darga dead so he can get the encryption keys to his accounts.
Adventure 5 (The First to Strike): Varth provides the intel on Project Leviathan’s location. He does this because the project designers (competent rivals) are in his way. He uses the heroes to clear the board.
Adventure 10 (Jaws of the Leviathan): The Turn. Varth contacts the heroes from the bridge of the Super Star Destroyer. He is wearing a pristine uniform. He reveals he traded the heroes and the Rebel fleet for his reinstatement.
The Reveal Line: "Don't look at me like that. I told you on day one: everyone has a price. The Emperor just met mine. Nothing personal, kid. Strictly business.".
Sample Dialogue:
"You want to stop the Empire? Cute. You can't stop a tidal wave, but if you're smart, you can learn to surf. I'm not asking you to die for the cause. I'm asking you to help me rob the biggest bank in the galaxy. Are you in, or are you going to stay poor and righteous?"


Adventure 2: A Wretched Hive
Part 1: The Shakedown
The Briefing: The Keys to the Kingdom The narrative begins aboard the heroes’ starship in hyperspace. The adrenaline of the prison break has faded, replaced by the quiet hum of the hyperdrive. Admiral Gilder Varth convenes a meeting in the common area. He has discarded his tattered prison uniform for whatever casual wear was available onboard, looking less like a military officer and more like a relaxed gambler counting his winnings.
Varth lays out the reality of their situation. He confirms that the data secured from "The Accountant" on Jakku provides the routing numbers for the embezzled Project Leviathan funds—a fortune massive enough to buy a moon. However, the accounts are biometrically locked and require a dual-authentication key. Varth holds one half; the other half is stored on a secure datapad held by his former partner in crime, Darga the Hutt.
Varth explains the complication: Darga believes Varth is dead or imprisoned forever. If Varth shows his face, Darga will kill him instantly to claim the full fortune. Therefore, the heroes must act as Varth's proxies. Their mission is to travel to Darga’s base of operations, infiltrate his inner circle, and steal the datapad containing the encryption keys. Varth provides them with the name of a low-level fixer who owes him a debt, a Devaronian named Warrick Raden, who can get them through the front door.
Arrival: The Other Side of the Lake The ship reverts from hyperspace above Takodana. In this era (16 BBY), Takodana is a lush, neutral haven in the Western Reaches, famous for the castle of the pirate queen Maz Kanata. However, Varth directs the heroes away from the castle. He explains that Darga the Hutt was banned from Maz’s court years ago for his "crude appetites" and violation of her neutral edicts.
Instead, the heroes land at Blackwind Point, a ramshackle smuggler's encampment on the opposite shore of Nymeve Lake. Unlike the festive atmosphere of Maz’s castle, Blackwind Point is a muddy, desperate shantytown populated by refugees fleeing the Imperial Pacification Campaigns and criminals too violent for civilized ports. The atmosphere is tense; the Empire has no official garrison here, but their spies are everywhere.
Scene 1: The Welcoming Committee As the heroes disembark, they are immediately accosted. This is a classic frontier shakedown. A swoop gang known as The Vipers circles the landing pad on repulsor-bikes, revving their engines to intimidate the newcomers. They are led by Kessra, a scarred human woman with a vibro-blade strapped to her leg.
Kessra demands a "landing tax" for protection, claiming that Darga the Hutt owns the airspace on this side of the lake. The heroes have a choice: pay the exorbitant fee, intimidate the gang, or draw their blasters. This encounter sets the tone for Takodana: on this side of the lake, might makes right. If the heroes fight and win, the Vipers retreat, and the local scavengers look at the heroes with newfound respect (and fear). If they pay, they are marked as easy marks for pickpockets and grifters in the camp.
Scene 2: Hunting the Fixer The heroes must track down Varth’s contact, Warrick Raden. The encampment is a maze of tents, grounded starships, and mud-brick hovels. The heroes must navigate the local underworld to find him.
Through conversation with local bartenders, refugees, or fences, the heroes learn that Raden has gone to ground. He is a nervous Devaronian who used to run errands for Darga but has recently vanished from the local cantinas. Rumor has it that Raden skimmed money from the Hutt and is terrified of Darga’s retribution. The locals point the heroes toward the Water Filtration Plant, a rusted industrial facility on the edge of the swamp where Raden is rumored to be squatting.
Scene 3: The Rat in the Trap The heroes approach the filtration plant, a noisy, steam-venting structure half-sunk in the bog. Inside, they find Warrick Raden hiding in a maintenance shed. He is a nervous wreck, clutching a blaster and jumping at shadows.
When the heroes approach, Raden initially mistakes them for Darga’s hitmen. The heroes must de-escalate the situation. Once they mention Varth’s name, Raden is shocked—he thought the Admiral was dead. He confirms that he can get them into Darga’s palace, but he refuses to go back there freely. He claims Darga has "changed" recently, becoming paranoid and obsessed with his new Imperial guests.
Scene 4: The Hit Squad Just as the heroes are negotiating with Raden, Darga’s actual hit squad arrives. Darga has grown tired of Raden’s absence and sent a team to silence him. The shed is besieged by Trandoshan Mercenaries (hired guns often used by the Empire and the Hutts for dirty work) and perhaps a heavy combat droid.
A chaotic firefight ensues in the cramped machinery of the filtration plant. Steam pipes burst, obscuring vision, and the Trandoshans use the environment to hunt the heroes. The heroes must protect Raden; if he dies, their easy route into the palace dies with him.
Scene 5: The Proposition Once the dust settles and the hit squad is defeated, Raden is terrified but grateful. He realizes he can no longer stay in the encampment. The heroes have the leverage now.
Raden explains the plan: Darga is currently hosting a delegation of "high rollers" and business partners. To keep them entertained, the Hutt is looking for fresh talent for his court. Raden agrees to present the heroes to Darga not as business partners (which would require vetting), but as "The Entertainment."
Depending on the heroes' skills, Raden suggests they pose as gladiators for Darga’s arena, high-stakes gamblers for his tables, or specialized mercenaries looking for work. He warns them that Darga is currently hosting members of the Imperial Mining Guild and that the heroes must keep their political affiliations hidden.
The Transition: Raden leads the heroes to a skiff on the edge of the lake. Across the mist-shrouded water, they see Darga’s Fortress—a moss-covered, ancient stone ruin that the Hutt has retrofitted with gaudy technology and heavy weaponry. It looms over the water like a beast waiting to be fed.
The heroes board the skiff, leaving the safety of the shore behind. They are heading into the lion's den to rob a Hutt in the middle of a dinner party.


Based on the "Revised Campaign Guide" details and the narrative structure of the Western Reaches campaign, here is the detailed narrative walkthrough for Adventure 2: A Wretched Hive, specifically Part 2B: The Court of the Crime Lord (Scenes 1 through 4).
Part 2: The Court of the Crime Lord
The Setup: Into the Mist The narrative picks up immediately following the heroes' meeting with the nervous fixer, Warrick Raden. Under the cover of Takodana’s perpetual twilight, the heroes board a repulsor-skiff piloted by the trembling Devaronian. They leave the relative safety of the smuggler’s encampment and head out across the dark, mist-shrouded waters of Nymeve Lake.
In the distance, Darga’s base of operations looms out of the fog. It is not a pristine palace, but a moss-covered, ancient stone fortress—a relic of a forgotten age that the Hutt has claimed and retrofitted with ugly, industrial blast doors and heavy repeating blaster turrets.
Admiral Varth chimes in over the heroes' encrypted comms from the safety of their ship. He reminds them of the stakes: Darga holds the Encryption Keys on a secure datapad that is never far from his person. Varth emphasizes that this is a "smash and grab" job. They are to get in, charm the Hutt long enough to swipe the data, and get out before Darga realizes his old partner is stealing back his own embezzled fortune. Varth warns them to follow Raden’s lead, but to be ready for the Hutt’s legendary paranoia.
Scene 1: The Audition (The Throne Room) The skiff docks at a rotting stone quay at the base of the fortress. The heroes are met by Darga's "welcoming committee"—a squad of pig-like Gamorrean guards wielding vibro-axes, led by a sleek, red-eyed Neimoidian named Igren Demos, Darga’s Majordomo. Demos eyes the group with suspicion but allows Raden to escort them inside.
The heroes are led through damp, torch-lit corridors that smell of mold and roasting meat, finally emerging into the Throne Room. The chamber is a sensory assault: thick smoke hangs in the air, a jizz-wailer band plays frantically in the corner, and the room is crowded with sycophants, thieves, and scavengers.
At the far end, resting on a massive reinforced hover-sled, sits Darga the Hutt. He is bloated, scarred, and flanked by a polished silver protocol droid, TC-663. Raden steps forward, bowing low, and presents the heroes as "prime talent"—specialists seeking work in the Western Reaches.
Darga is not easily impressed. He barks in Huttese, his deep voice shaking the room. TC-663 translates: “The Mighty Darga is bored with beggars. Prove you are worth his air.”
This initiates the Audition. Darga demands a demonstration of the heroes' talents immediately.
The Muscle: A warrior hero might be ordered to face off against one of Darga's elite bodyguards or a captive beast in the center of the room.
The Face: A scoundrel or noble might be challenged to a high-stakes game of chance or a battle of wits to prove they aren't undercover law enforcement.
The Tech: A slicer or mechanic might be told to fix a piece of failing tech on Darga’s hover-sled while the Hutt watches, a blaster aimed at their head.
If the heroes succeed, Darga laughs—a wet, booming sound—and welcomes them as "guests" (essentially prisoners with privileges). However, he turns his gaze to Warrick Raden. Darga decides he is tired of the fixer’s fidgeting and failures. He orders the Gamorreans to drag Raden to the Dungeons to "learn silence." As Raden screams and is hauled away, Darga smiles at the heroes, a clear warning of the price of failure.
Scene 2: The Long Con (The Court) Over the next standard day cycle, the heroes must navigate the dangerous politics of the court to get close to Darga’s datapad. Varth stays on the comms, coaching them, pointing out high-value targets, and reminding them to keep their eyes on the prize. The heroes must mingle, gather intel, and wait for their opening.
During this time, three key events occur that allow the heroes to move freely or gather crucial lore:
The Iron Ring Delegation: A group of Trandoshan Slavers storms into the court, dragging chained Nazren prisoners (hulking aliens from the Nizon system). They get into a heated argument with Darga over payment. Listening in, the heroes learn that Darga is a key supplier of slave labor for Project Leviathan. The Empire is paying Darga in raw resources, which he is fencing. This confirms Varth’s story: Darga is neck-deep in Imperial business.
The Imperial Buyer: A woman arrives, radiating authority despite her civilian clothes. This is Lady Sinde, a logistics officer for the Empire. She ignores the scum of the court and speaks privately with Darga. Varth recognizes her voice over the comms. He realizes she is here to arrange the next shipment of raw materials for the Leviathan construction site. This raises the stakes: the Empire is physically present in the fortress.
The Droid Race (The Distraction): To celebrate his new deal with Lady Sinde, Darga announces a Droid Race in the fortress’s lower courtyard. Guests are given control visors to pilot battered astromechs through a lethal obstacle course. This is the heroes' best chance. While Darga is distracted gambling on the race and shouting at the pilots, the heroes can slip away from the main party to slice the datapad or explore the secure areas of the fortress.
Scene 3: The Complication (The Dungeon) Using the cover of the Droid Race, the heroes slip into the restricted lower levels of the fortress. Varth directs them to look for the Main Vault, where Darga might keep backups of the encryption keys. Instead of a vault, they find a high-security detention block guarded by elite droids.
Inside the most secure cell, they do not find credits. They find a stasis pod hooked up to life-support machinery. Inside is a human woman in a Force-induced coma: Jedi Master Denia.
Darga found her wreckage after Order 66 and has kept her as a trophy, hoping to auction her off to the Inquisitorius for a massive bounty. She is weak, barely alive, and sensing the heroes' presence, she feebly reaches out through the Force.
The Moral Event Horizon: Admiral Varth realizes what they have found via the heroes' audio feeds. His voice turns cold and hard.
"Leave her. Do not touch that console. She is a liability. If you wake a Jedi, every Inquisitor in the sector will hunt us down. We are here for the money, not to be martyrs. Get the keys and get out."
The heroes face a critical choice: follow the plan and act like the scoundrels they claim to be, or risk everything to save a relic of the Old Republic.
Scene 4: The Turn (The Alarm) The climax of this act is triggered by the heroes' decision in the dungeon or a slip-up in the throne room.
If they try to rescue Denia: Disconnecting her from the stasis machinery triggers a silent alarm. More dangerously, Igren Demos, who is Force-sensitive, feels the sudden disturbance in the Force as the Jedi Master wakes. He immediately alerts Darga.

Adventure 3: The Queen of Air and Darkness

The Setup: The Fire Sale The adventure begins aboard the heroes’ starship as it travels through hyperspace toward the Anoat Sector. The tension from the escape at Takodana has settled, but Admiral Gilder Varth calls a meeting in the common area. He looks pleased with himself, flipping a credit chip over his knuckles.

Varth’s Pitch:
"We hurt him bad. Darga is bleeding credits. He’s meeting an Imperial buyer at the Cloud City Sabacc Tournament to sell off tons of stolen gas. The Empire brings the cash; Darga brings the gas codes. The Empire gets no gas, Darga gets no money, and we get paid twice. Simple."
Arrival: The Boomtown The ship reverts to realspace above Bespin. In 16 BBY, Cloud City is not yet the polished resort of Lando Calrissian. Scene 1: The Brick Wall The heroes dock at the Cloudscape Platforms. Their first goal is to locate Darga or his proxy to figure out how the handover will happen.

The Encounter: The Ugnaughts admit they sent the threat to scare the heroes away, hoping to prevent a gang war. They hate Darga. They reveal that the Hutt is bullying the Guild, forcing Ugnaught riggers to siphon military-grade gas directly to Imperial ships, bypassing the Guild's quotas.
The Deal: The Ugnaughts offer a trade. If the heroes can stop Darga’s thugs from stealing a shipment right now, the Ugnaughts will tell them exactly how the handover at the tournament is structured.
Scene 3: The Refinery Rumble The heroes travel to Refinery Platform 13, a suspended industrial platform hanging dangerously deep in the gas giant's atmosphere. The wind howls, and steam vents obscure vision.
The Enemy: They find a squad of Trandoshan Mercenaries (Darga’s muscle) and corrupt Ugnaught riggers loading gas canisters onto a shuttle.
The Hazards: This is a tactical combat encounter. The fight takes place on catwalks over a lethal drop. Stray blaster shots risk rupturing gas tanks (creating explosion hazards) or steam pipes (creating scald hazards).
The Objective: Defeat the Trandoshans and secure the platform.
The Intel: A datapad found on the Trandoshan leader confirms the gas is destined for the Imperial Navy and mentions a ship called the Shackles of Nizon.
Scene 4: The Imperial Mark Returning to the Guild office, Fyren and Krygg pay up. They reveal that Darga is obsessed with security and refuses to meet the buyer in private. He insisted the handover happen in plain sight during the Cloud City Sabacc Tournament.
The Ugnaughts point the heroes to the Yerith Bespin Hotel, where the high-rollers are staying.
The Stakeout: The heroes infiltrate the hotel lobby. Amidst the alien eccentrics, they spot a man who doesn't fit: Lieutenant Armen Arandis.
The Target: Arandis is dressed in civilian luxury, but he moves with military stiffness. He is flanked by "bodyguards" who are clearly undercover Stormtroopers.
Varth's Insight: Varth chimes in via comms. "That's the Bagman. Arandis. A mathematical genius from the Academy. He isn't here to play cards; he's here to transfer the credits to Darga's proxy by losing intentionally. We need to be at that table."

The Cheat: Tech heroes might infiltrate the droid maintenance crew to rig the dealer droids.

"That ship is the 'clean-up crew.' If Arandis loses the money, or if Darga tries to double-cross them, Draco won't bother with arrests. He’ll glass the city to bury the evidence. You need to win that pot and get the gas codes before Draco gets impatient."

The Setting: The Royal Casino The Royal Casino is the jewel of Cloud City’s tourist district, but in this era (16 BBY), it lacks the refined elegance of the later Calrissian administration. Roleplaying the Investigation:

The Cheat (Silas Draver): The heroes notice a flamboyant human gambler, Silas Draver, arguing with a pit boss. Draver is loud, arrogant, and seemingly drunk, but a hero watching closely (Perception DC 20) notices he is stone cold sober. He is casing the room, checking the sightlines of the security cameras.

Scene 2: The Pressure Cooker (Day 3)

The Imperial Reaction: Arandis doesn't flinch. He stares Szygar down. The final day. Only five players remain: Lt. Arandis, Lady Fioro, Silas Draver, a random alien (e.g., Creeska the Rodian), and The Hero (if they survived).

Enemies: Silas Draver (Scoundrel Boss), 4 Thugs, 3 Weaponized Dealer Droids.

Scene 2: The Hunter in the Wires The Entrance: The heavy quiet is broken by the slow, deliberate clack-clack-clack of boot steps against the grating. A slow, mocking applause echoes from the shadows of the server banks. The Visual: Vril Vrakth, the Zabrak bounty hunter, steps into the pale light. He doesn't look like a man here for a bounty; he looks like an executioner who has already been paid. He leans against a terminal, casually checking the thermal coil on his heavy blaster. Vrakth: "Personally? I think he meant 'Goodbye.' The Admiral hates loose ends. And frankly, splitting the payout with you wasn't in my contract." The Reality: Varth didn’t just abandon them; he paid to have them buried here so he wouldn't have to share the glory or the Imperial pardon. The Violence: Vrakth doesn't monologue further. He moves with terrifying, explosive speed. The room instantly turns into a slaughterhouse of ricocheting blaster fire and thermal detonators. The Collateral: The fight shreds the room. Sparks shower like rain as terminals melt into slag. The physical Drift Data banks are reduced to molten glass and twisted metal. No one else will ever extract data from this room. But Varth already has his copy.
Scene 3: The Bleeding Path (The Race Back) The Escape: With the hunter bleeding out on the grated floor—or buried under collapsed server towers—the heroes must flee. The Ascent: The facility alarms are finally screaming. The riot from the surface has breached the upper levels. The pristine white corridors are now choked with smoke, panicked Imperial technicians, and looting rebels. The Sprint: The heroes must push through the chaos, their lungs burning, armor scorched and heavy. They burst out of the blast doors back into Martyr's Plaza. The air is thick with ash. They have to run miles through the burning streets of Junker’s Flats, back to the red clay badlands where they left the Glorious Chariot. Every step is a race against the sound of starship engines warming up in the distance.
Scene 4: The Judas Flight (The Betrayal)
The Setting: The heroes navigate the dimly lit, refined cavern systems that honeycomb the cliffs at the edge of the city. The air smells of damp stone and the sharp tang of refined starship fuel. They are exhausted, bleeding, and gasping for breath as they approach the terminal concourse overlooking Docking Bay 7.
The Sight: Through the thick, blast-proof plasteel viewing ports of the concourse, the windswept cliff-side platform is visible. The heroes' ship sits on the pad. Resting adjacent to it is the sleek, predatory silhouette of an Imperial Shuttle.
The Transaction: Standing on the tarmac is Admiral Varth, but he is transformed. Gone is the worn, ragged coat of a desperate exile. He is clad in a pristine, immaculately pressed Imperial uniform, his rank cylinders gleaming on his chest. Beside him, an Imperial Captain scrolls through a glowing datapad—the Drift Charts. The Captain gives a sharp nod. The purchase is complete. Varth has bought his life back.
The Interaction: Through the thick plasteel, Varth’s eyes lock onto the heroes standing in the concourse shadows. There is no shock, no guilt. Only the cold, calculating stare of a man tying up loose ends. The heroes raise their weapons, but the heavy plasteel viewing ports render any shot impossible. They are forced to watch.
The Escalation: Varth murmurs something to the Captain and gestures toward the concourse. The Captain barks an order. From the auxiliary bays, a terrifyingly large contingent of Stormtroopers marches out—a wall of white armor and heavy repeating blasters moving to lock down the cavern entrances. It is not a skirmish force; it is an execution squad. An overwhelming, unwinnable wall of Imperial muscle.
The Spiteful Blow: As the troops deploy, Varth turns his attention to his wrist. He taps a sequence into his Bracer Comp. Outside, the heroes' ship violently erupts. A massive fireball consumes the vessel, the blistering shockwave rattling the plasteel glass and sending a shock of heat into the concourse. Metal rains down onto the landing pad.
The Departure: Bathed in the orange glow of the burning wreckage, Varth looks back to the heroes one last time. He raises a hand, casually tipping his pristine officer’s cap in a mock salute. Then, he turns his back and strides up the ramp of the Imperial Shuttle.
The Status: The shuttle's repulsorlifts whine, kicking up a storm of ash and burning debris as it climbs into the dark sky. The heroes are trapped behind the plasteel, their ship is a burning skeleton of slag, and an overwhelming legion of Stormtroopers is flooding the cavern system to wipe them out. They have the data, but they have lost everything else.

Scene 5: Ash and Silence (The Epilogue)
The Retreat: The cavern walls echo with the synchronized, marching thud of the Imperial execution squad. The heroes must abandon the blazing wreckage of their ship and disappear into the unrefined, pitch-black lava tubes beneath Junker's Flats. Bruised and empty-handed, they emerge hours later into the smoldering ruins of the city, seeking refuge in the fortified basements held by Sartok and the bleeding remnants of the insurrection.
The Exodus: Over the next few days, the sky above Malpaz changes. The Arkanian Dragon, the massive Star Destroyer that cast a permanent shadow over the city, breaks orbit. With Varth having successfully transmitted the Drift Data directly to Grand Moff Tarkin, the planet's value as a forward observational research facility is reduced to zero. Imperial landing craft strip the garrison of its vital equipment and troops, abandoning the pacification effort. The Empire hasn't lost the war for Malpaz; they simply stopped playing on a board that no longer mattered.
The Spark: Three days pass. The heroes are grounded in a lawless, starving city, desperately slicing into abandoned comms towers trying to hail a smuggler or a freighter. Suddenly, a heavily encrypted frequency overrides their makeshift console. It isn't Imperial, and it isn't Varth. It is a tight-beam transmission from a vessel broadcasting the transponder codes of a deep-space explorational ship.
The Hologram: The flickering blue image of Master Denia projects from the console. She is clad in fresh, utilitarian robes. She looks weary, but the desperation is gone, replaced by a cold, sharpened focus. Denia: "This is Denia. I apologize for the silence. I needed time to heal... and to find allies. Look to the northern ridge."
The Rescue: Slipping through the planet's diminished sensor net, a Nebulon-B Frigate drops through the ash clouds. The Resurgence. Its hull is scarred, bearing the faded, painted-over heraldry of the Old Republic. To the heroes, it looks like a ghost ship crewed by stubborn holdouts who refuse to admit the regime has fallen. (They do not know this is an active cell of Operation Fulcrum, under the command of Captain Verana; to them, it is simply a ride off this rock).
The Revelation (Aboard the Resurgence): Once safely aboard, the heroes meet with Denia. The reality of their situation, and the galaxy's, is laid bare. Varth didn't just steal a map; he stole the key to the galaxy's last safe havens. The Stakes: Varth now possesses both the Jedi Holocrons and the Imperial Drift Data. Combined, these artifacts provide unprecedented, stable navigation routes into the Unknown Regions—lanes that even the most daring smugglers don't know exist. The Threat: If Varth solidifies his position with the Empire, the Western Regions—currently a wild, lawless frontier where the Empire is barely a rumor—will be flooded with Star Destroyers. The Empire will dominate the hidden space lanes, suffocating the last free corners of the galaxy. The Prize: Alternatively, for those operating in the shadows, those navigational aids are worth a hundred lifetimes of credits.
The Conclusion: The heroes have a ship, a direction, and a vendetta. Varth has the keys to the galaxy, and they are the only ones who know he has them. The hunt moves from the mud of Malpaz to the stars.


Adventure 6: The Core of Corruption (Eriadu)
Part 1: The Quiet Rebellion (The Insertion) The heroes bypass the orbital blockades and plunge into the bruised, toxic orange atmosphere of Eriadu. The sky is choked with the exhaust of ten thousand weapons factories, and acid rain smears their viewport. Tarkin’s capital is a jagged, brutalist expanse of black armor-plating and glowing red refineries.
Captain Verana has provided a contact, but finding them is a slow, tense process. Eriadu is not a battlefield; it is a cage. Tarkin’s punishment for sedition is swift and complete, leaving the populace terrified to even make eye contact. The local resistance does not fight—they operate strictly as a quiet logistical hub, funneling resources to the Western Reaches. When the heroes finally track the cell down to a decaying, lower-level sump, the operatives are terrified. The heroes must navigate a tense negotiation, convincing these frightened rebels to risk the wrath of the Empire to provide forged clearance codes, worker uniforms, and a path into the Central Processing Citadel.
Part 2: The Glass Bridge (The Tableau) Disguised and carrying forged credentials, the heroes breach the lower levels of Tarkin’s Citadel. They ascend through the sterile, freezing corridors of the Imperial bureaucracy, bypassing standard security patrols. As they reach the upper administrative levels, they must cross a massive, enclosed transparisteel walkway bridging two of the Citadel's spires.
Across a vast, wind-whipped expanse of the city, they have a clear line of sight into the grand, open-air balcony of Grand Moff Tarkin's personal office. The howling storm outside muffles all sound, leaving the scene playing out in chilling, absolute silence.
Standing before a black polished desk is Admiral Varth. His posture is rigid, submissive—a gambler betting his life on what he brings to the table. Standing beside him is Valin Draco, the Inquisitor’s face now half-covered in jagged, dark metallic cybernetics from their last encounter. Behind Draco stands a lithe, shadowed figure: his Apprentice.
At the window stands Grand Moff Tarkin, his uniform impeccable. There is a brief, wordless exchange. Draco leans in to whisper to Tarkin. Tarkin nods slowly, turning to his desk. He slides a datapad and a command cylinder across the glass to Varth—the keys to his new command. He dismisses them with a flick of his wrist. Varth and Draco turn and leave. The Apprentice follows a moment later.
Tarkin does not watch them go. He clasps his hands behind his back and walks out onto his balcony, rain hissing against his boots. He raises a crystal glass of amber liquid to his lips. And then, slowly, his head turns. Across the vast, impossible expanse of the city, his eyes lock directly onto the heroes on the glass bridge. He doesn't reach for an alarm. He simply takes a sip, holding their gaze. To him, they are not a threat; they are merely insects crawling on his window.
Part 3: The Panicked Slice (The Descent) The heavy silence of the bridge is shattered by the shrieking wail of the Citadel's general alarms. Tarkin didn't ignore them; he simply let the automated defenses deal with the vermin.
Master Denia—operating as their "guy in the chair" from a stealth-baffled comms array in the lower sumps—cuts into their earpieces. "They're locking down the grid. You have minutes before the blast doors seal. I sense... a cold darkness moving toward your level. Hurry."
The heroes must fight their way through waves of standard Imperial Stormtroopers. Their objective is the Primary Data Center. Varth just used the Citadel's systems to legitimize his new command, and the Imperial bureaucracy copies everything. The heroes must slice the central terminal to download the transit orders and figure out exactly where Varth is taking his new route data.
Part 4: The Dark Apprentice (The Extraction) The heroes breach the Primary Data Center—a massive room filled with row upon row of glowing server racks. The download begins, the progress bar crawling as red emergency lights pulse.
Then, the blast doors hiss open. It isn't just more Stormtroopers. A squad of elite Death Troopers—clad in specialized black armor, their comms crackling with encrypted bursts of static—files into the room. Stepping from between their ranks is Draco's Apprentice, igniting a crimson lightsaber.
A brutal fight ensues amid the server banks. The heroes must hold off the terrifying speed of the Apprentice and the overlapping fire of the black-armored troopers just long enough for the download to finish. Once the datapad chimes, the heroes must execute a desperate escape. With the Citadel entering total lockdown and the air venting from the room, they shoot out the transparisteel viewport and rappel down the side of the spire into the orange smog, fleeing back into the shadows.
Epilogue: The Short Breath Exhausted, scorched by blaster fire, and clutching the data drive containing Varth's destination, the heroes make it back to the resistance safehouse in the lower sumps. Denia is waiting for them.
They have the data, but the victory is hollow, and the rest is non-existent. The campaign instantly transitions into the setup for Adventure 7. The Empire is ruthlessly efficient on Eriadu. Outside the reinforced iron doors of the safehouse, the sounds of Imperial probe droids and marching boots are already echoing through the tunnels. The Empire has found them.
