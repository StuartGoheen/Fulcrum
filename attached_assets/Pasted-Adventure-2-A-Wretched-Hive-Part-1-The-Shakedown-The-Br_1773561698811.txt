Adventure 2: A Wretched Hive
Part 1: The Shakedown
The Briefing: The Keys to the Kingdom The narrative begins aboard the heroes’ starship in hyperspace. The adrenaline of the prison break has faded, replaced by the quiet hum of the hyperdrive. Admiral Gilder Varth convenes a meeting in the common area. He has discarded his tattered prison uniform for whatever casual wear was available onboard, looking less like a military officer and more like a relaxed gambler counting his winnings.
Varth lays out the reality of their situation. He confirms that the data secured from "The Accountant" on Jakku provides the routing numbers for the embezzled Project Leviathan funds—a fortune massive enough to buy a moon. However, the accounts are biometrically locked and require a dual-authentication key. Varth holds one half; the other half is stored on a secure datapad held by his former partner in crime, Darga the Hutt.
Varth explains the complication: Darga believes Varth is dead or imprisoned forever. If Varth shows his face, Darga will kill him instantly to claim the full fortune. Therefore, the heroes must act as Varth's proxies. Their mission is to travel to Darga’s base of operations, infiltrate his inner circle, and steal the datapad containing the encryption keys. Varth provides them with the name of a low-level fixer who owes him a debt, a Devaronian named Warrick Raden, who can get them through the front door.
Arrival: The Other Side of the Lake The ship reverts from hyperspace above Takodana. In this era (16 BBY), Takodana is a lush, neutral haven in the Western Reaches, famous for the castle of the pirate queen Maz Kanata. However, Varth directs the heroes away from the castle. He explains that Darga the Hutt was banned from Maz’s court years ago for his "crude appetites" and violation of her neutral edicts.
Instead, the heroes land at Blackwind Point, a ramshackle smuggler's encampment on the opposite shore of Nymeve Lake. Unlike the festive atmosphere of Maz’s castle, Blackwind Point is a muddy, desperate shantytown populated by refugees fleeing the Imperial Pacification Campaigns and criminals too violent for civilized ports. The atmosphere is tense; the Empire has no official garrison here, but their spies are everywhere.
Scene 1: The Welcoming Committee As the heroes disembark, they are immediately accosted. This is a classic frontier shakedown. A swoop gang known as The Vipers circles the landing pad on repulsor-bikes, revving their engines to intimidate the newcomers. They are led by Kessra, a scarred human woman with a vibro-blade strapped to her leg.
Kessra demands a "landing tax" for protection, claiming that Darga the Hutt owns the airspace on this side of the lake. The heroes have a choice: pay the exorbitant fee, intimidate the gang, or draw their blasters. This encounter sets the tone for Takodana: on this side of the lake, might makes right. If the heroes fight and win, the Vipers retreat, and the local scavengers look at the heroes with newfound respect (and fear). If they pay, they are marked as easy marks for pickpockets and grifters in the camp.
Scene 2: Hunting the Fixer The heroes must track down Varth’s contact, Warrick Raden. The encampment is a maze of tents, grounded starships, and mud-brick hovels. The heroes must navigate the local underworld to find him.
Through conversation with local bartenders, refugees, or fences, the heroes learn that Raden has gone to ground. He is a nervous Devaronian who used to run errands for Darga but has recently vanished from the local cantinas. Rumor has it that Raden skimmed money from the Hutt and is terrified of Darga’s retribution. The locals point the heroes toward the Water Filtration Plant, a rusted industrial facility on the edge of the swamp where Raden is rumored to be squatting.
Scene 3: The Rat in the Trap The heroes approach the filtration plant, a noisy, steam-venting structure half-sunk in the bog. Inside, they find Warrick Raden hiding in a maintenance shed. He is a nervous wreck, clutching a blaster and jumping at shadows.
When the heroes approach, Raden initially mistakes them for Darga’s hitmen. The heroes must de-escalate the situation. Once they mention Varth’s name, Raden is shocked—he thought the Admiral was dead. He confirms that he can get them into Darga’s palace, but he refuses to go back there freely. He claims Darga has "changed" recently, becoming paranoid and obsessed with his new Imperial guests.
Scene 4: The Hit Squad Just as the heroes are negotiating with Raden, Darga’s actual hit squad arrives. Darga has grown tired of Raden’s absence and sent a team to silence him. The shed is besieged by Trandoshan Mercenaries (hired guns often used by the Empire and the Hutts for dirty work) and perhaps a heavy combat droid.
A chaotic firefight ensues in the cramped machinery of the filtration plant. Steam pipes burst, obscuring vision, and the Trandoshans use the environment to hunt the heroes. The heroes must protect Raden; if he dies, their easy route into the palace dies with him.
Scene 5: The Proposition Once the dust settles and the hit squad is defeated, Raden is terrified but grateful. He realizes he can no longer stay in the encampment. The heroes have the leverage now.
Raden explains the plan: Darga is currently hosting a delegation of "high rollers" and business partners. To keep them entertained, the Hutt is looking for fresh talent for his court. Raden agrees to present the heroes to Darga not as business partners (which would require vetting), but as "The Entertainment."
Depending on the heroes' skills, Raden suggests they pose as gladiators for Darga’s arena, high-stakes gamblers for his tables, or specialized mercenaries looking for work. He warns them that Darga is currently hosting members of the Imperial Mining Guild and that the heroes must keep their political affiliations hidden.
The Transition: Raden leads the heroes to a skiff on the edge of the lake. Across the mist-shrouded water, they see Darga’s Fortress—a moss-covered, ancient stone ruin that the Hutt has retrofitted with gaudy technology and heavy weaponry. It looms over the water like a beast waiting to be fed.
The heroes board the skiff, leaving the safety of the shore behind. They are heading into the lion's den to rob a Hutt in the middle of a dinner party.

Part 2: The Court of the Crime Lord
The Setup: Into the Mist The narrative picks up immediately following the heroes' meeting with the nervous fixer, Warrick Raden. Under the cover of Takodana’s perpetual twilight, the heroes board a repulsor-skiff piloted by the trembling Devaronian. They leave the relative safety of the smuggler’s encampment and head out across the dark, mist-shrouded waters of Nymeve Lake.
In the distance, Darga’s base of operations looms out of the fog. It is not a pristine palace, but a moss-covered, ancient stone fortress—a relic of a forgotten age that the Hutt has claimed and retrofitted with ugly, industrial blast doors and heavy repeating blaster turrets.
Admiral Varth chimes in over the heroes' encrypted comms from the safety of their ship. He reminds them of the stakes: Darga holds the Encryption Keys on a secure datapad that is never far from his person. Varth emphasizes that this is a "smash and grab" job. They are to get in, charm the Hutt long enough to swipe the data, and get out before Darga realizes his old partner is stealing back his own embezzled fortune. Varth warns them to follow Raden’s lead, but to be ready for the Hutt’s legendary paranoia.
Scene 1: The Audition (The Throne Room) The skiff docks at a rotting stone quay at the base of the fortress. The heroes are met by Darga's "welcoming committee"—a squad of pig-like Gamorrean guards wielding vibro-axes, led by a sleek, red-eyed Neimoidian named Igren Demos, Darga’s Majordomo. Demos eyes the group with suspicion but allows Raden to escort them inside.
The heroes are led through damp, torch-lit corridors that smell of mold and roasting meat, finally emerging into the Throne Room. The chamber is a sensory assault: thick smoke hangs in the air, a jizz-wailer band plays frantically in the corner, and the room is crowded with sycophants, thieves, and scavengers.
At the far end, resting on a massive reinforced hover-sled, sits Darga the Hutt. He is bloated, scarred, and flanked by a polished silver protocol droid, TC-663. Raden steps forward, bowing low, and presents the heroes as "prime talent"—specialists seeking work in the Western Reaches.
Darga is not easily impressed. He barks in Huttese, his deep voice shaking the room. TC-663 translates: “The Mighty Darga is bored with beggars. Prove you are worth his air.”
This initiates the Audition. Darga demands a demonstration of the heroes' talents immediately.
The Muscle: A warrior hero might be ordered to face off against one of Darga's elite bodyguards or a captive beast in the center of the room.
The Face: A scoundrel or noble might be challenged to a high-stakes game of chance or a battle of wits to prove they aren't undercover law enforcement.
The Tech: A slicer or mechanic might be told to fix a piece of failing tech on Darga’s hover-sled while the Hutt watches, a blaster aimed at their head.
If the heroes succeed, Darga laughs—a wet, booming sound—and welcomes them as "guests" (essentially prisoners with privileges). However, he turns his gaze to Warrick Raden. Darga decides he is tired of the fixer’s fidgeting and failures. He orders the Gamorreans to drag Raden to the Dungeons to "learn silence." As Raden screams and is hauled away, Darga smiles at the heroes, a clear warning of the price of failure.
Scene 2: The Long Con (The Court) Over the next standard day cycle, the heroes must navigate the dangerous politics of the court to get close to Darga’s datapad. Varth stays on the comms, coaching them, pointing out high-value targets, and reminding them to keep their eyes on the prize. The heroes must mingle, gather intel, and wait for their opening.
During this time, three key events occur that allow the heroes to move freely or gather crucial lore:
The Iron Ring Delegation: A group of Trandoshan Slavers storms into the court, dragging chained Nazren prisoners (hulking aliens from the Nizon system). They get into a heated argument with Darga over payment. Listening in, the heroes learn that Darga is a key supplier of slave labor for Project Leviathan. The Empire is paying Darga in raw resources, which he is fencing. This confirms Varth’s story: Darga is neck-deep in Imperial business.
The Imperial Buyer: A woman arrives, radiating authority despite her civilian clothes. This is Lady Sinde, a logistics officer for the Empire. She ignores the scum of the court and speaks privately with Darga. Varth recognizes her voice over the comms. He realizes she is here to arrange the next shipment of raw materials for the Leviathan construction site. This raises the stakes: the Empire is physically present in the fortress.
The Droid Race (The Distraction): To celebrate his new deal with Lady Sinde, Darga announces a Droid Race in the fortress’s lower courtyard. Guests are given control visors to pilot battered astromechs through a lethal obstacle course. This is the heroes' best chance. While Darga is distracted gambling on the race and shouting at the pilots, the heroes can slip away from the main party to slice the datapad or explore the secure areas of the fortress.
Scene 3: The Complication (The Dungeon) Using the cover of the Droid Race, the heroes slip into the restricted lower levels of the fortress. Varth directs them to look for the Main Vault, where Darga might keep backups of the encryption keys. Instead of a vault, they find a high-security detention block guarded by elite droids.
Inside the most secure cell, they do not find credits. They find a stasis pod hooked up to life-support machinery. Inside is a human woman in a Force-induced coma: Jedi Master Denia.
Darga found her wreckage after Order 66 and has kept her as a trophy, hoping to auction her off to the Inquisitorius for a massive bounty. She is weak, barely alive, and sensing the heroes' presence, she feebly reaches out through the Force.
The Moral Event Horizon: Admiral Varth realizes what they have found via the heroes' audio feeds. His voice turns cold and hard.
"Leave her. Do not touch that console. She is a liability. If you wake a Jedi, every Inquisitor in the sector will hunt us down. We are here for the money, not to be martyrs. Get the keys and get out."
The heroes face a critical choice: follow the plan and act like the scoundrels they claim to be, or risk everything to save a relic of the Old Republic.
Scene 4: The Turn (The Alarm) The climax of this act is triggered by the heroes' decision in the dungeon or a slip-up in the throne room.
If they try to rescue Denia: Disconnecting her from the stasis machinery triggers a silent alarm. More dangerously, Igren Demos, who is Force-sensitive, feels the sudden disturbance in the Force as the Jedi Master wakes. He immediately alerts Darga.
If they try to slice the Datapad: If the team upstairs tries to snag the keys from Darga during the race and fails, the Hutt catches them red-handed.
Suddenly, the fortress goes into Lockdown. Klaxons scream through the stone corridors. In the Throne Room, Darga screams for the heroes' heads.
"Spies! They are Varth's ghosts! Kill them all!"
Darga’s hover-sled engages its repulsors, and he begins to flee toward his private hangar bay, clutching the datapad with the keys. The heroes in the dungeon find their exit blocked by blast doors sealing shut, while the heroes in the court are suddenly surrounded by Gamorreans and Trandoshan mercenaries. The heist is blown. It is no longer a sneak job; it is a fight for survival and a race to catch the Hutt before he escapes off-world with the fortune.
Scene 5: The Majordomo’s Stand The fortress is in chaos. Klaxons blare, and the heavy blast doors sealing the lower levels begin to grind shut. Darga has already fled the throne room, retreating toward his private hangar. However, the path to the exit is not clear.
Blocking the heroes' escape—whether they are emerging from the dungeon secret passage with the comatose Jedi or fighting their way across the main floor—is Igren Demos, the Neimoidian Majordomo. He is no longer the simpering servant. He stands flanked by his elite Trandoshan Bodyguards, wielding a vibro-staff with surprising lethality.
The Reveal: If the heroes have rescued Master Denia, Demos’s eyes widen. He senses her presence in the Force. He reveals his true nature—he is Force-sensitive (untrained) and intends to take the Jedi himself to bargain with the Inquisitorius, cutting Darga out of the deal.
The Conflict: This is the "Boss Fight" of the location. Demos uses minor Force powers (Force Slam, Push) to separate the heroes or knock them into the environment (braziers, statues).
Varth’s Commentary: Admiral Varth screams over the comms, furious that the heroes are bogged down fighting a "middle-manager" while the real target (Darga and the Encryption Keys) is getting away. "Stop playing with the help! He’s stalling you! Darga is leaving!"
Scene 6: The Hangar Bay Defeating Demos allows the heroes to sprint for the hangar bay. They burst through the blast doors into the humid, open-air cavern Darga uses as a docking bay.
The Sight: They are too late to stop the launch. Darga’s massive, gaudy luxury yacht, the Glorious Chariot, is already hovering on repulsors, engines flaring blue-white. The boarding ramp is retracted.
The Imperial Complication: To make matters worse, Lady Sinde (the Imperial Logistics Officer) is not fleeing. She has ordered her escort of Stormtroopers to secure the hangar and eliminate all witnesses to preserve the secrecy of Project Leviathan.
The Action: The heroes must fight their way across the open hangar floor, using crates of Tibanna gas and supplies for cover, to reach their own ship (or the skiff they arrived in). They are pinned down by heavy repeater fire from the Stormtroopers while Darga’s yacht blasts out of the hangar tunnel, collapsing part of the ceiling behind him to discourage pursuit.
The Chase (Cinematic Moment): If the heroes are quick, they might scramble to their ship and launch. They burst out of the waterfall-obscured cave entrance into the twilight skies of Takodana. They see Darga’s yacht rocketing toward the upper atmosphere.
The Escape: Before they can lock weapons, Darga’s yacht jumps to hyperspace. He is gone.
Conclusion: The Aftermath The heroes are safe in hyperspace (or hiding in the asteroid field of the system), tending to their wounds. The mood is tense.
The Failure (The Keys): They failed to secure the Encryption Keys. Darga has escaped with the datapad. Without it, Varth cannot access the embezzled billions.
The Success (The Trail): Varth, frantically working the ship’s sensors during the escape, managed to get a partial trajectory lock on Darga’s yacht before it jumped.
Varth: "He was panicked. He didn't plot a random course; he went for a bolt-hole. The vector aligns with the Anoat Sector. There's only one place a slug like Darga would feel safe and wealthy enough to hide there: Bespin. Cloud City."
The Moral Weight (If Denia was saved): If the heroes saved Master Denia, she lies in the med-bay, still weak. Varth is furious. He berates the heroes for bringing a "heat magnet" on board. He warns them that dragging a Jedi Master around is a death sentence; the Inquisitors will hunt them relentlessly now.
The Twist: Denia wakes up briefly. She doesn't thank them. She looks at Varth with recognition and distrust, then looks at the heroes. "You are walking a dark path with a treacherous guide. But the Force has brought us together for a reason." She then falls into a natural sleep.
The Setup for Adventure 3: The heroes are now broke (having missed the payday), wanted by the Empire (after the shootout with Sinde's troops), and burdened with a Jedi Master. Their only option is to chase Darga to Cloud City, infiltrate his operation there, and steal the keys before he vanishes for good.
This concludes Adventure 2 and leads directly into Adventure 3: The Queen of Air and Darkness.
I. Major NPCs (The Narrative Pillars)
These characters drive the central conflict. Darga is the target of the heist, and Denia is the "Moral Event Horizon" that determines the future of the campaign.
Jedi Master Denia: The Echo of the Republic
Role: The "MacGuffin" turned Moral Compass.
Visuals: An older human woman, prematurely aged by years of stasis and drug-induced sleep. She wears tattered, stained Jedi robes that look like rags. When she wakes, her eyes are fierce and piercing, contrasting with her frail physical state.
Backstory (16 BBY Context): Denia was a Jedi General during the Clone Wars. During the chaos of Order 66, she was shot down over Takodana (replacing Cato Neimoidia). Darga found her wreckage. Realizing a live Jedi was worth a fortune to the right buyer, he placed her in a stasis pod in his dungeon, keeping her sedated while he looked for a buyer rich enough (and quiet enough) to take her. She has been trapped in her own mind for three years, sensing the darkness falling over the galaxy.
Personality:
Disoriented but Wise: She doesn't know the Empire exists yet. She speaks with the authority of the Old Republic, which feels jarring and archaic in the gritty "Young Empire" era.
The Unwanted Conscience: To Varth, she is a liability. To the heroes, she represents a choice. If they wake her, she immediately senses their potential (or lack thereof). She doesn't beg; she commands. "The Force has not abandoned this galaxy, even if you have."
Role in the Heist: She is the wrench in the gears. Varth orders the heroes to leave her because she will bring down the Inquisitorius. Denia communicates telepathically with the heroes before they even reach her cell, whispering to their consciences. Saving her ruins the "clean getaway" but secures a powerful ally against the Sith.
Darga the Hutt: The Wannabe Warlord
Role: The Mark / The Greedy Partner.
Visuals: A massive, bloated Hutt with a distinct, deep scar running down his left side (a souvenir from a rival). He drips with gaudy jewelry—rings on every finger, gold chains, and a localized repulsor-sled plated in electrum. He smokes expensive cigars and constantly eats live squids from a bowl.
Backstory: Darga was Admiral Varth's silent partner in the embezzlement of Project Leviathan. When Varth vanished (captured), Darga assumed Varth was dead and tried to seize the full assets. He is currently "laundering" Imperial resources (Tibanna gas and slaves) to build his own criminal empire on the fringe, using Takodana’s neutrality as a shield.
Personality:
Paranoid Hedonist: He is terrified that Varth is still alive or that the Empire will cut him loose. He masks this fear with excessive cruelty and gluttony.
The "Sophisticate": Unlike Jabba, who embraces his gangster nature, Darga tries to act like a civilized noble. He demands elaborate court etiquette and becomes enraged if treated like a common criminal. He refuses to speak Basic, forcing everyone to use his translator, TC-663, to maintain an air of superiority.
Role in the Heist: He physically holds the datapad with the Encryption Keys on his person (or his sled) at all times. He is the obstacle the heroes must charm, trick, or rob. He eventually flees to Cloud City, leading to Adventure 3.
II. Secondary NPCs (The Heist Mechanics)
These characters are crucial to the specific events of A Wretched Hive but may not carry over to the rest of the campaign (with the exception of Lady Sinde/Draco).
Igren Demos: The Treacherous Majordomo
Role: The Mini-Boss / The Actual Threat.
Description: A tall, sleek Neimoidian in expensive robes. He is Darga’s right hand but despises the Hutt.
Motivation: He wants to take over Darga’s operation. He is secretly Force-sensitive (untrained) and has contacted Inquisitor Draco, offering to trade Master Denia in exchange for Imperial support in his coup. He is the one who locks the palace down when the heroes try to escape.
Campaign Impact: He serves as the physical boss fight of the adventure, wielding a vibrostaff and minor dark side powers.
Lady Sinde: The Imperial Connection
Role: The Reality Check.
Description: A severe human woman in civilian clothes that cost more than the heroes' ship. She radiates authority.
Motivation: She is a logistics officer for Project Leviathan. She is at the palace to negotiate the shipment of slaves and Tibanna gas. She views Darga as a disgusting but necessary tool.
Campaign Impact: Seeing her confirms Varth’s story: The Empire is building something huge, and they are here on the edge of the galaxy to fund it. If the heroes cross her, she marks them for the ISB.
TC-663 ("Six-Six"): The Inside Man
Role: The Spy.
Description: A silver protocol droid, polished to a mirror sheen. He is polite, subservient, and seemingly harmless.
Secret: He is actually an agent of Switch (the droid crime lord from Adventure 1). He hates Darga. If the heroes treat him with respect (or use Switch’s code phrase), he will provide them with maps of the palace and the location of the datapad.

These characters flesh out the world and provide specific hurdles during the heist.

Gome: The Moneychanger
Role: The Info Dump.
Description: An elderly Duros accountant who lives in the palace. He doesn't care about the crime; he just likes the numbers. He can explain exactly what Darga is shipping to the Empire (Tibanna Gas for ship weapons) if the heroes corner him.

Adventure 3: The Queen of Air and Darkness
Part 1: The Floating World Heist
The Setup: The Fire Sale The adventure begins aboard the heroes’ starship as it travels through hyperspace toward the Anoat Sector. The tension from the escape at Takodana has settled, but Admiral Gilder Varth calls a meeting in the common area. He looks pleased with himself, flipping a credit chip over his knuckles.
Varth confirms that because the heroes successfully stole the Encryption Keys in the previous adventure, Darga the Hutt is effectively locked out of the embezzled fortune. However, the Hutt isn't beaten yet. Varth explains that Darga has been stockpiling military-grade Spin-Sealed Tibanna Gas on Cloud City. Now that Darga is broke and panicked, he is liquidating his physical inventory in a desperate "fire sale" to the Empire.
Varth’s Pitch:
"We hurt him bad. Darga is bleeding credits. He’s meeting an Imperial buyer at the Cloud City Sabacc Tournament to sell off tons of stolen gas. The Empire brings the cash; Darga brings the gas codes. We are going to intercept that deal. We enter the tournament, we win the pot—which is actually the Imperial payment—and we leave the Hutt with nothing. The Empire gets no gas, Darga gets no money, and we get paid twice. Simple."
Arrival: The Boomtown The ship reverts to realspace above Bespin. In 16 BBY, Cloud City is not yet the polished resort of Lando Calrissian. It is a rough-and-tumble mining boomtown, a "Wild West" in the clouds populated by prospectors, gamblers, and fugitives fleeing the Core. The Empire is present, but they tread lightly to avoid disrupting the vital gas production.
Scene 1: The Brick Wall The heroes dock at the Cloudscape Platforms. Their first goal is to locate Darga or his proxy to figure out how the handover will happen.
The Investigation: The heroes hit the streets. They find the tourist district is packed for the upcoming tournament, but the underworld is tight-lipped. Darga has bribed the Wing Guard (local police) and port officials to erase his footprint.
The Threat: While investigating a casino or cantina, the heroes’ datapads beep. They receive a text-only message, unsigned: "Stop digging. The drop is long, and the gas pressure is high. Go home."
The Trace: Varth (acting as "The Man in the Chair" from the ship) or a tech-savvy hero traces the signal. It didn't come from Darga’s yacht; it came from the Industrial District, specifically the offices of the Parliament of Guilds (the Ugnaught labor leadership).
Scene 2: The Industrial Connection The heroes descend into The Works—the noisy, steam-filled industrial bowels of the city. They confront the source of the message: Fyren and Krygg, two terrified Ugnaught administrators.
The Encounter: The Ugnaughts admit they sent the threat to scare the heroes away, hoping to prevent a gang war. They hate Darga. They reveal that the Hutt is bullying the Guild, forcing Ugnaught riggers to siphon military-grade gas directly to Imperial ships, bypassing the Guild's quotas.
The Deal: The Ugnaughts offer a trade. If the heroes can stop Darga’s thugs from stealing a shipment right now, the Ugnaughts will tell them exactly how the handover at the tournament is structured.
Scene 3: The Refinery Rumble The heroes travel to Refinery Platform 13, a suspended industrial platform hanging dangerously deep in the gas giant's atmosphere. The wind howls, and steam vents obscure vision.
The Enemy: They find a squad of Trandoshan Mercenaries (Darga’s muscle) and corrupt Ugnaught riggers loading gas canisters onto a shuttle.
The Hazards: This is a tactical combat encounter. The fight takes place on catwalks over a lethal drop. Stray blaster shots risk rupturing gas tanks (creating explosion hazards) or steam pipes (creating scald hazards).
The Objective: Defeat the Trandoshans and secure the platform.
The Intel: A datapad found on the Trandoshan leader confirms the gas is destined for the Imperial Navy and mentions a ship called the Shackles of Nizon.
Scene 4: The Imperial Mark Returning to the Guild office, Fyren and Krygg pay up. They reveal that Darga is obsessed with security and refuses to meet the buyer in private. He insisted the handover happen in plain sight during the Cloud City Sabacc Tournament.
The Ugnaughts point the heroes to the Yerith Bespin Hotel, where the high-rollers are staying.
The Stakeout: The heroes infiltrate the hotel lobby. Amidst the alien eccentrics, they spot a man who doesn't fit: Lieutenant Armen Arandis.
The Target: Arandis is dressed in civilian luxury, but he moves with military stiffness. He is flanked by "bodyguards" who are clearly undercover Stormtroopers.
Varth's Insight: Varth chimes in via comms. "That's the Bagman. Arandis. A mathematical genius from the Academy. He isn't here to play cards; he's here to transfer the credits to Darga's proxy by losing intentionally. We need to be at that table."
Scene 5: The Buy-In (Preparation) The heroes realize they cannot attack Arandis in the hotel (too much security). They have to hit him during the game.
The Entry: The tournament buy-in is massive (10,000 credits). Varth provides the stake using funds from previous jobs, but warns them: "Don't lose it. It's an investment, not a gift."
The Roles: The heroes must decide how to infiltrate the tournament for Part 2.
The Player: One hero (high Deception/Wisdom) enters as a competitor to intercept the credits.
The Security: Martial heroes can get hired by the casino as extra security guards to watch for Darga’s enforcers.
The Cheat: Tech heroes might infiltrate the droid maintenance crew to rig the dealer droids.
The Cliffhanger: The Shadow Arrives As the heroes finalize their entry, their comlinks chirp. It is Varth, and his voice is tight with genuine fear.
"We have a problem. A massive energy spike just hit the system edge. An Imperial Star Destroyer just dropped out of hyperspace. And it's not just any ship—I know that transponder code. It’s the Assiduous."
Varth explains that the Assiduous is the flagship of Inquisitor Valin Draco. The Empire isn't just here to buy gas; they are here to ensure the Project Leviathan secret is kept.
"That ship is the 'clean-up crew.' If Arandis loses the money, or if Darga tries to double-cross them, Draco won't bother with arrests. He’ll glass the city to bury the evidence. You need to win that pot and get the gas codes before Draco gets impatient."
Here is the detailed narrative walkthrough for Adventure 3, Part 2: The High Stakes Game.
This section covers the multi-day span of the Cloud City Sabacc Tournament. It is designed to build tension slowly, transforming a card game into a high-stakes espionage thriller, culminating in a chaotic blaster fight.
Part 2: The High Stakes Game
The Setting: The Royal Casino The Royal Casino is the jewel of Cloud City’s tourist district, but in this era (16 BBY), it lacks the refined elegance of the later Calrissian administration. It is a loud, smoky, and aggressive establishment where prospectors gamble away fortunes in tibanna gas futures and desperate refugees bet their last credits on a lucky draw.
For the duration of the tournament, the casino has been locked down. Security is tight. Wing Guards in blue uniforms patrol the perimeter, but the interior security is handled by the casino’s owner, Jacc Mandelbrot, and his private security force (which the heroes may have infiltrated).
Admiral Varth monitors the situation from the heroes' ship, parked at a safe distance or in a rented hangar. He is the voice in their ears, constantly analyzing the probability of the Assiduous vaporizing them if they fail.
Scene 1: The Elimination Rounds (Days 1 & 2)
The tournament begins with over fifty competitors. The goal here is not just to win cards, but to identify Darga’s Proxy and the Imperial Bagman.
The Atmosphere: The air is thick with distinct aromas—Corellian whiskey, Ryll spice smoke, and ozone from the dealer droids. The sound is a constant, low roar of conversation, punctuated by the digital chirps of credit chips being transferred and the mechanical whir of the shuffling droids.
Roleplaying the Investigation:
The Gambler (Player Character): If a hero is playing, describe the grueling mental fatigue. They aren't just playing cards; they are playing against species with telepathy, cybernetic implants, and alien poker faces.
The Security (Muscle Character): Heroes posing as guards catch glimpses of the seedy underbelly. They notice smugglers slipping vibro-blades into boots, or spice deals happening in the refresher stations.
The Observer (Face/Tech Character): Heroes in the crowd must dodge pickpockets and drunk prospectors while trying to get close enough to the high-rollers tables to scan faces or intercept comms chatter.
Key Encounters & Clues: Over the first two days, the field whittles down. Varth helps analyze the players via cam-link.
The Imperial (Lieutenant Arandis): The heroes spot Lt. Armen Arandis at Table 4. He plays like a machine—cold, mathematical, and emotionless. He never drinks, never smiles, and stacks his chips in perfectly symmetrical towers.
Varth’s Comms: "Look at him. He’s not gambling; he’s doing arithmetic. He’s waiting for the hand-off. Watch who he loses to. He’s not allowed to lose to anyone but Darga’s proxy."
The Cheat (Silas Draver): The heroes notice a flamboyant human gambler, Silas Draver, arguing with a pit boss. Draver is loud, arrogant, and seemingly drunk, but a hero watching closely (Perception DC 20) notices he is stone cold sober. He is casing the room, checking the sightlines of the security cameras.
The Proxy (Lady Fioro): A Hapan noblewoman, Lady Mirana Fioro, dominates her table. She is icy, dismissive, and plays with a ruthlessness that bankrupts a weeping Dug prospector in the first hour.
The Connection: A hero succeeding on a Sense Motive/Insight check notices she keeps glancing at Arandis across the room, not with attraction, but with calculation.
Scene 2: The Pressure Cooker (Day 3)
By the third day, only two tables remain. The buy-ins have escalated, and the casual observers have turned into a rapt audience. The tension in the room shifts from "party" to "funeral."
Event: The Devaronian Incident Midway through the session, a Devaronian gambler named Szygar (a minor NPC) goes bust against Lt. Arandis. Szygar snaps. He leaps up, accusing the Imperial of cheating.
The Action: Szygar draws a hold-out blaster. Panic ripples through the crowd.
The Hero’s Moment: If heroes are working security, this is their test. They must subdue Szygar without causing a panic that cancels the tournament. If they are players, they must decide whether to intervene or let Arandis handle it.
The Imperial Reaction: Arandis doesn't flinch. He stares Szygar down. If the heroes don't act, Arandis’s "bodyguards" (undercover Stormtroopers) will vaporize the Devaronian, potentially causing the casino to close for an investigation. Varth screams in their ears: "Don't let them shoot! If the cops shut us down, we lose the money!"
The Result: The heroes must de-escalate or take Szygar down quietly. Doing so earns them the trust of the casino owner, Jacc Mandelbrot, giving them freer movement on the final day.
Scene 3: The Final Table (Day 4)
The final day. Only five players remain: Lt. Arandis, Lady Fioro, Silas Draver, a random alien (e.g., Creeska the Rodian), and The Hero (if they survived).
The Setup: The main floor is cleared. A single table sits in the center under a harsh spotlight. The rest of the casino is in shadow, packed with hundreds of silent spectators.
The Hand: The game is Sabacc. The pot is enormous—over 500,000 credits.
The Imperial Play: Arandis is playing strangely. He folds on strong hands and raises on weak ones, specifically when playing against Lady Fioro.
Varth’s Realization: "He’s dumping the chips. He’s transferring the payment to her pot legally. She’s the Proxy."
The Signal (The Climax of the Game): It comes down to Arandis vs. Fioro. Arandis goes "All In." Fioro calls.
The Reveal: Arandis reveals a losing hand. Fioro wins the massive pot. The crowd erupts.
The Code: Instead of sweeping his cards away, Arandis deliberately arranges them on the table surface in a specific order: The One of Coins, The Three of Sabers, The Seven of Flasks.
Varth’s Decode: Varth sees the cards through the hero’s eyes/camera.
Varth: "That's not a hand. 1... 3... 7... That's a frequency. 137.12. It’s the slave circuit control code for the ship Darga is buying. He just handed her the keys to the getaway car and the money to pay for the gas."
The Objective: The transaction is complete. Lady Fioro has the credits (digitally transferred to a datapad) and the code memorized. The heroes must grab her before she leaves.
Scene 4: The Heist (Combat)
Just as the heroes move to intercept Lady Fioro, Silas Draver makes his move. He wasn't playing to win; he was playing to be close to the money.
The Turn: Draver stands up, pulls a heavy blaster pistol, and shoots the Casino Owner, Mandelbrot, or fires into the ceiling.
Draver: "Nobody moves! The Empire isn't the only one getting paid today!"
The Chaos:
The Droids: Draver presses a remote. The three Sabacc Dealer Droids (floating mechanical dealers) spin around, revealing hidden blaster cannons. They open fire on the security guards and the crowd.
The Thugs: Draver’s men, disguised as waiters and guests, pull weapons. They secure the exits.
The Objective: Draver grabs Lady Fioro, holding a blaster to her head. He demands the datapad with the credit transfer.
The Combat Encounter: The heroes are caught in a three-way crossfire in a crowded casino.
Enemies: Silas Draver (Scoundrel Boss), 4 Thugs, 3 Weaponized Dealer Droids.
Hazards: Panicking civilians (difficult terrain), overturned tables (cover), and the Dealer Droids have flight and 360-degree firing arcs.
Varth’s Input: "Forget the civilians! Get the datapad! If Draver gets away with the money, Darga walks!"
The Imperial Complication: Lt. Arandis attempts to intervene, drawing a concealed military-grade blaster. He fights effectively but is focused on killing Draver to protect the secrecy of the transaction. He likely dies in the crossfire (or is killed by Draver), removing the only Imperial witness.
Scene 5: The Aftermath
The heroes defeat Draver and his thugs. The casino is a wreck. Sirens wail in the distance—the Wing Guard is coming.
The Interrogation: Lady Fioro is wounded in the fight (a graze or debris). She is terrified. The heroes have moments to get the info before the police arrive.
The Intimidation: She realizes Darga has abandoned her.
The Reveal: She spills the location.
Fioro: "He’s at the lower docks! Docking Bay 4414. He has the Shackles of Nizon holding in orbit on slave circuits. He’s taking the gas and the slaves and leaving the sector tonight!"
The Cliffhanger: Varth scans the system.
Varth: "I'm picking up a transmission from the surface. Fioro sent the code before you grabbed her. The Shackles of Nizon just activated its engines. And... damn it. The Assiduous just entered the system. Draco is here. Get to the hangar. If Darga launches that ship, we lose everything."
Transition: The heroes must flee the crime scene, racing through the panic of Cloud City to reach the industrial sector before Darga escapes. This leads directly to Part 3: The Hangar Bay Showdown.
Here is the comprehensive, highly detailed narrative walkthrough for Adventure 3, Part 3: The Hangar Bay Showdown.
This narrative incorporates the Western Reaches setting adjustments (specifically targeting Project Leviathan in the Unknown Regions instead of the Deep Core), the "Con Man" persona of Admiral Varth, and the specific betrayal plot involving Captain Okeefe.
Part 3: The Hangar Bay Showdown
The Setup: The Burn Notice The heroes stand amidst the wreckage of the Royal Casino. The Sabacc table is overturned, credit chips are scattered like confetti, and the smell of ozone from blaster fire hangs heavy in the air. In the distance, the wail of sirens grows louder—the Bespin Wing Guard is closing the net.
Admiral Varth chimes in over the heroes' encrypted comms. He is aboard the heroes' own starship, holding a sensor-masked orbit near the city's industrial underside. His voice is tight, stripping away his usual casual charm to reveal the cold tactician beneath.
"Listen to me. The local police feed just lit up; they have your descriptions. But the Wing Guard is the least of your problems. My sensors just picked up a massive energy signature dropping out of hyperspace. It's the Assiduous. Inquisitor Draco isn't taking chances. He just launched a full wing of TIE Fighters, and they aren't vectoring for the city—they're heading for the industrial docks."
Varth explains the grim reality: Draco is scrubbing the mission. He intends to destroy Darga the Hutt, the slave ship, and anyone else who can link the Empire to the embezzlement of Project Leviathan.
"Draco is cutting his losses. He’s going to blow Darga's yacht out of the sky to bury the evidence. You need to get to Docking Bay 4414 immediately. If Darga launches, the Empire shoots him down, and we lose the credits, the codes, and the trail to Leviathan."
Scene 1: The Gauntlet (Streets of Cloud City) The journey to the lower docks is a sprint through a city in lockdown. Darga has called in every favor he has left, bribing corrupt elements of the Wing Guard to declare the heroes "Enemy Saboteurs."
The Atmosphere: The chase transitions from the pristine, white corridors of the luxury district into the steam-filled, rusted catwalks of the Industrial District (The Works). Tibanna gas vents hiss, obscuring vision, and the orange glow of the Bespin sunset filters through heavy industrial grates.
The Obstacles: The heroes are pinned down by Elite Bespin Security Guards and a squad of Stormtroopers deployed from a shuttle to cut off their retreat. This is a running battle. If the heroes stop to fight every enemy, they will be overwhelmed. They must use the environment—shooting steam pipes to scald pursuers, jumping onto moving cargo skiffs, or cutting through maintenance ducts.
Varth’s Overwatch: Varth acts as "The Man in the Chair," hacking door controls to keep paths open. "Left at the junction! No, the other left! I'm jamming their comms, but I can't stop them from shooting at you. Move!"
Scene 2: Hutt's Hangar (Docking Bay 4414) The heroes kick open the blast doors to Docking Bay 4414. This cavernous hangar is open to the roiling clouds of Bespin on one side, the wind howling through the magnetic field.
The Prize: Dominating the center is Darga’s personal luxury yacht, the Glorious Chariot. It is a garish, gold-and-purple modified SoroSuub Luxury 3000. Its engines are priming, whining with a high-pitched turbine squeal that vibrates in the heroes' teeth.
The Escort: Idling on the deck are six Z-95 Headhunters painted in the colors of the Mionne Hutt Cartel. Pilots are scrambling up the ladders into their cockpits.
The Hutt: Darga the Hutt stands at the base of the ramp, screaming at his loading crews to move faster. He is flanked by a heavy Security Droid. When he sees the heroes, his fear turns to rage. He bellows in Huttese: "Kill them! They brought the Inquisitor down on us! I want their heads!"
Scene 3: The Firefight The heroes must fight across the open hangar floor, using fuel crates and maintenance machinery for cover.
The Objective: Stop the Glorious Chariot from launching.
The Tactical Threat: The Z-95 pilots are trying to take off. If they get airborne, they will strafe the hangar interior, turning the floor into a kill zone. Savvy heroes might shoot the pilots on the ground to hijack the fighters for themselves.
The Boss: As the heroes push forward, Darga realizes his mercenaries are falling. He retreats up the ramp of the Glorious Chariot. The heroes must breach the ship or disable its engines from the outside. If they board, they fight Darga in the narrow, opulent corridors of his own yacht, where his massive bulk is a deadly weapon.
Scene 4: Grand Theft Starship With Darga defeated (captured or killed) and the hangar secured, Varth barks a warning:
"Imperial stormtroopers have breached the elevator shaft! You have thirty seconds before a platoon comes through that door. You can't go back the way you came. Take the ships! Steal the yacht! I’ll rendezvous with you in orbit!"
The Loot: The heroes commandeer the Glorious Chariot (a Colossal space transport with decent shields and weapons) and any intact Z-95 Headhunters.
The Escape: As the hangar doors blast open and stormtroopers pour in, the heroes punch the throttles. They blast out of the hangar bay, leaving the pursuing troops behind, and fly directly into the orange clouds of Bespin.
Scene 5: Skirmish Over Bespin As the heroes break the atmosphere and reach orbit, the sensor board lights up with a chaotic tactical display. The situation is dire.
The Threat: The Imperial Star Destroyer Assiduous hangs in high orbit, launching a screen of TIE Fighters.
The Target: The Shackles of Nizon (the Gallofree Medium Transport filled with slaves) is drifting dead in space.
The Trap: A massive, automated Tibanna Gas Hauler has been hacked by the Empire. It is burning hard on a collision course with the slave ship. The Empire intends to ram the transport, vaporizing the evidence of Project Leviathan.
The "Cold Calculus" of Admiral Varth: Before the heroes can react, Admiral Varth hails them from their original ship, safely holding at the system's edge. His voice is calm, professional, and utterly cold.
"Good work getting airborne. I see the situation up there. Draco is scrubbing the mission; that Gas Hauler is going to impact the transport in less than two minutes. It’s a tragedy, but it’s not our tragedy. We have the credits, we have the data. Plotting jump coordinates for the rendezvous point... now. Spool up the hyperdrives. Let's get out of here while we’re rich."
The Interruption: The Voice in the Dark Suddenly, a frantic transmission cuts through the static. It is Captain Okeefe, broadcasting from the bridge of the Shackles of Nizon.
"Varth! Varth, come in! I'm on the transport! I've secured the bridge, but the slave circuits are locked out! I can't steer, and sensors show a collision imminent! Where is that backup code? I need a little help here!"
The Betrayal Revealed: The heroes realize the truth instantly. Varth didn't send Okeefe to secure the ship; he sent her there to be destroyed along with the evidence. He views her as a loose end. When the heroes confront him or hesitate, Varth sighs, sounding annoyed rather than guilty.
"She knew the risks. Look, if we turn back, that Star Destroyer will range us. We have the money. We have the Leviathan data. We jump. Now."
Scene 6: The Choice The heroes must decide: Follow Varth and leave Okeefe to die, or turn and fight. Assuming they choose to act as heroes:
The Dogfight: The heroes (in the Glorious Chariot and Z-95s) must engage the TIE Fighters screening the Gas Hauler.
The Objective: Destroy the Tibanna Gas Hauler before it impacts the slave ship. It is a massive target with heavy shielding, requiring concentrated fire.
The "Varth Intervention": As the heroes engage the TIEs, they find themselves outgunned. TIEs swarm their position. Just as shields are failing, laser fire erupts from the flank, vaporizing a TIE fighter on a hero's tail.
It is Varth. He has brought their original ship around for an attack run. He is furious.
"Blast it all! I am not losing my 50% cut because you idiots decided to be martyrs! I am engaging the TIEs. You focus on that hauler! And I am billing you for the fuel!"
GM Note: This preserves Varth’s cover. He isn't saving them out of altruism; he is saving them because they are the only ones who can retrieve the fortune for him. He needs them alive—for now.
The Climax: With Varth holding off the TIEs, the heroes pour fire into the Tibanna Gas Hauler. It explodes in a massive fireball just meters from the slave ship.
Okeefe: "That's it! Slave circuits fried. Nav-computer is green. We are jumping to the rendezvous... and Varth? You and I are going to have a very long talk."
The Shackles of Nizon vanishes into hyperspace.
Conclusion: The Escape The Assiduous begins to fire turbolasers, green bolts flashing past the heroes' cockpits. Varth screams over the comms:
"We overstayed our welcome! Punch it!"
The heroes, Varth, and the stolen fighters jump to hyperspace.
Aftermath (End of Act I) The heroes arrive at a safe rendezvous point.
The Confrontation: Okeefe is furious, punching Varth in the jaw. Varth takes it, smoothing his jacket and claiming it was a "tactical calculation that changed."
The Rewards:
The Ship: The heroes now possess the Glorious Chariot (a Luxury 3000) and potentially Z-95 fighters.
The Credits: They have the tournament winnings and Darga’s liquid assets.
The Data: They have the Project Leviathan files.
The Tease: Varth wipes the blood from his lip and looks at the datapad. "This isn't just a smuggling operation. This data points to a massive construction project. But it's not in the Core. It's in the Unknown Regions. If you want to know what the Empire is really building, we have to go off the map."
