Generated Backstory
Joric Thane grew up on the arid plains of Ryloth, a human among Twi'leks fighting for their home. His childhood was not marked by play, but by the distant thrum of gunships and the acrid scent of blaster fire carried on the wind. Water was always rationed, and the faces of the Clone Troopers and later Imperial soldiers who cycled through the outposts were the only constant in a world defined by conflict. His body developed an unnatural awareness, a twitching readiness that pulled him away from danger before his mind even registered the threat. He had learned to dodge stray fire and collapsing debris long before he ever learned to read a datapad, his reflexes honed by the brutal weather of war.
The Imperial pacification campaigns on Ryloth escalated, turning the world Joric knew into a charnel house. He was aboard a crowded refugee transport attempting to slip past the blockade when a sudden, blinding flash ripped through the hull, tearing the vessel apart. Joric remembered the sickening lurch, the roar of depressurization, and the chilling silence that followed as he was flung into the void, a solitary speck among the debris. He clung to a piece of wreckage for days, his lungs burning, his limbs numb, his body somehow refusing to give in even as his mind screamed for release. That catastrophe branded a grim endurance into his very bones, teaching him to function far beyond the limits of exhaustion and pain.
The memory of that wreck, and the burning settlements he witnessed on Ryloth, clung to Joric like a shroud. The whine of a repulsor engine, the smell of ozone after a blaster discharge, the hiss of a blast door — any of these could pull him back to the terror of that day, leaving him silent and shaking. When genuine mortal danger presented itself, his body would move with a terrifying precision, dodging and weaving through chaos, but the aftermath always left him disoriented, with hours he could not account for. He rarely spoke above a murmur, his voice lacking any command, and never carried a blaster, having long ago found himself clumsy and ineffective with ranged weapons, preferring the cold steel of a close encounter.
Now, Joric drifted through the Western Reaches, a silent shadow haunting the spires and canyons of Batuu, his unique humming blade flashing with deadly grace in the cramped alleys. He found himself drawn to those caught in impossible binds, often risking his own neck to free them, though he once secured passage for a single traumatized youth by providing information that led to the capture of an entire cargo of other fugitives. His mind sometimes mapped his opponents' movements with an unnatural clarity that surprised even him, allowing him to disarm and disable them with terrifying fluidity. He never learned to lie convincingly, his face betraying him instantly, and he abhorred the clumsy weight of heavy weaponry, constant reminders of the artillery that defined his youth. The whispers of the Force, an unsettling prescience, sometimes guided his choices, a dangerous secret he guarded fiercely, forever pursued by the echoes of his past.
