Joric Thane knew the smell of war before he knew the taste of sweetfruit. His earliest memories were of the dust-choked plains of Ryloth, where the distant thrum of starfighters and the crackle of blaster fire were as constant as the twin suns. He was a slight, observant child who learned to read the shift in the wind and the subtle tremors of the ground, his body moving without conscious thought to avoid falling debris or the sudden impact of an artillery shell. This instinctive, almost preternatural evasion kept him alive through the Clone Wars and the subsequent Imperial Pacification Campaigns that ravaged his homeworld.

The constant conflict culminated in a catastrophe that shattered his young life. Joric was aboard a civilian transport attempting to slip through an Imperial blockade above Ryloth, a vessel he had hoped would carry him to safety, when it was struck by stray fire. The hull buckled, decompression alarms shrieked, and the ship tore itself apart around him. He clawed his way through mangled durasteel and the freezing void, a solitary figure among countless dead, his body pushing past pain and exhaustion. Days later, when a salvage crew found his escape pod, he was barely conscious, the experience of surviving against impossible odds having seared an unnatural resilience into his very bones; he had also found a peculiar, silent blade tucked amidst the wreckage, a relic he sensed was meant for him.

The trauma of that wreck, layered upon years of war, settled deep within him. Now, the sudden hiss of a blast door, the distant whine of a repulsor engine, or the sharp tang of ozone after an energy discharge could plunge him back into the chaos, his body seizing with an ancient memory. He had once tried to pull a family from the burning wreckage of a downed shuttle, giving up his own chance at an early evacuation to drag the smallest children to safety, only to watch Imperial patrols close in. Later, to secure passage off a besieged outpost for himself and a small band of survivors, he had to leave behind a larger group of refugees, a choice that still haunted him, the faces of the condemned etched into his memory alongside the peculiar hum of his found blade, which he had since learned to wield with a frightening precision.

Now, in 16 BBY, Joric moved through the shadowy fringes of the Western Reaches, a gaunt figure often found in the neutral zones of Takodana. He survived by his wits and his blade, taking on work that required a precise touch and an ability to disappear, often retrieving lost cargo or extracting individuals from tight spots. During a particularly tense negotiation in Maz Kanata’s castle, when two rival gangs suddenly drew weapons, he saw the patterns of the impending brawl before it erupted, his mind effortlessly charting escape routes and disarming possibilities, a flash of insight that surprised even him. He kept his lightsaber hidden, a glow rod his only visible light source, always aware that the galaxy had little patience for his kind of power, and even less for his kind of past. He always seemed to find himself near those trapped, a silent question lingering in his every action: did someone escape a sealed fate because of him?
