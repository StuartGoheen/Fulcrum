Part 3: The Hangar Bay Showdown
The Setup: The Burn Notice The heroes stand amidst the wreckage of the Royal Casino. The Sabacc table is overturned, credit chips are scattered like confetti, and the smell of ozone from blaster fire hangs heavy in the air. In the distance, the wail of sirens grows louder—the Bespin Wing Guard is closing the net.
Admiral Varth chimes in over the heroes' encrypted comms. He is aboard the heroes' own starship, holding a sensor-masked orbit near the city's industrial underside. His voice is tight, stripping away his usual casual charm to reveal the cold tactician beneath.
"Listen to me. The local police feed just lit up; they have your descriptions. But the Wing Guard is the least of your problems. My sensors just picked up a massive energy signature dropping out of hyperspace. It's the Assiduous. Inquisitor Draco isn't taking chances. He just launched a full wing of TIE Fighters, and they aren't vectoring for the city—they're heading for the industrial docks."
Varth explains the grim reality: Draco is scrubbing the mission. He intends to destroy Darga the Hutt, the slave ship, and anyone else who can link the Empire to the embezzlement of Project Leviathan.
"Draco is cutting his losses. He’s going to blow Darga's yacht out of the sky to bury the evidence. You need to get to Docking Bay 4414 immediately. If Darga launches, the Empire shoots him down, and we lose the credits, the codes, and the trail to Leviathan."
Scene 1: The Gauntlet (Streets of Cloud City) The journey to the lower docks is a sprint through a city in lockdown. Darga has called in every favor he has left, bribing corrupt elements of the Wing Guard to declare the heroes "Enemy Saboteurs."
The Atmosphere: The chase transitions from the pristine, white corridors of the luxury district into the steam-filled, rusted catwalks of the Industrial District (The Works). Tibanna gas vents hiss, obscuring vision, and the orange glow of the Bespin sunset filters through heavy industrial grates.
The Obstacles: The heroes are pinned down by Elite Bespin Security Guards and a squad of Stormtroopers deployed from a shuttle to cut off their retreat. This is a running battle. If the heroes stop to fight every enemy, they will be overwhelmed. They must use the environment—shooting steam pipes to scald pursuers, jumping onto moving cargo skiffs, or cutting through maintenance ducts.
Varth’s Overwatch: Varth acts as "The Man in the Chair," hacking door controls to keep paths open. "Left at the junction! No, the other left! I'm jamming their comms, but I can't stop them from shooting at you. Move!"
Scene 2: Hutt's Hangar (Docking Bay 4414) The heroes kick open the blast doors to Docking Bay 4414. This cavernous hangar is open to the roiling clouds of Bespin on one side, the wind howling through the magnetic field.
The Prize: Dominating the center is Darga’s personal luxury yacht, the Glorious Chariot. It is a garish, gold-and-purple modified SoroSuub Luxury 3000. Its engines are priming, whining with a high-pitched turbine squeal that vibrates in the heroes' teeth.
The Escort: Idling on the deck are six Z-95 Headhunters painted in the colors of the Mionne Hutt Cartel. Pilots are scrambling up the ladders into their cockpits.
The Hutt: Darga the Hutt stands at the base of the ramp, screaming at his loading crews to move faster. He is flanked by a heavy Security Droid. When he sees the heroes, his fear turns to rage. He bellows in Huttese: "Kill them! They brought the Inquisitor down on us! I want their heads!"
Scene 3: The Firefight The heroes must fight across the open hangar floor, using fuel crates and maintenance machinery for cover.
The Objective: Stop the Glorious Chariot from launching.
The Tactical Threat: The Z-95 pilots are trying to take off. If they get airborne, they will strafe the hangar interior, turning the floor into a kill zone. Savvy heroes might shoot the pilots on the ground to hijack the fighters for themselves.
The Boss: As the heroes push forward, Darga realizes his mercenaries are falling. He retreats up the ramp of the Glorious Chariot. The heroes must breach the ship or disable its engines from the outside. If they board, they fight Darga in the narrow, opulent corridors of his own yacht, where his massive bulk is a deadly weapon.
Scene 4: Grand Theft Starship With Darga defeated (captured or killed) and the hangar secured, Varth barks a warning:
"Imperial stormtroopers have breached the elevator shaft! You have thirty seconds before a platoon comes through that door. You can't go back the way you came. Take the ships! Steal the yacht! I’ll rendezvous with you in orbit!"
The Loot: The heroes commandeer the Glorious Chariot (a Colossal space transport with decent shields and weapons) and any intact Z-95 Headhunters.
The Escape: As the hangar doors blast open and stormtroopers pour in, the heroes punch the throttles. They blast out of the hangar bay, leaving the pursuing troops behind, and fly directly into the orange clouds of Bespin.
Scene 5: Skirmish Over Bespin As the heroes break the atmosphere and reach orbit, the sensor board lights up with a chaotic tactical display. The situation is dire.
The Threat: The Imperial Star Destroyer Assiduous hangs in high orbit, launching a screen of TIE Fighters.
The Target: The Shackles of Nizon (the Gallofree Medium Transport filled with slaves) is drifting dead in space.
The Trap: A massive, automated Tibanna Gas Hauler has been hacked by the Empire. It is burning hard on a collision course with the slave ship. The Empire intends to ram the transport, vaporizing the evidence of Project Leviathan.
The "Cold Calculus" of Admiral Varth: Before the heroes can react, Admiral Varth hails them from their original ship, safely holding at the system's edge. His voice is calm, professional, and utterly cold.
"Good work getting airborne. I see the situation up there. Draco is scrubbing the mission; that Gas Hauler is going to impact the transport in less than two minutes. It’s a tragedy, but it’s not our tragedy. We have the credits, we have the data. Plotting jump coordinates for the rendezvous point... now. Spool up the hyperdrives. Let's get out of here while we’re rich."
The Interruption: The Voice in the Dark Suddenly, a frantic transmission cuts through the static. It is Captain Okeefe, broadcasting from the bridge of the Shackles of Nizon.
"Varth! Varth, come in! I'm on the transport! I've secured the bridge, but the slave circuits are locked out! I can't steer, and sensors show a collision imminent! Where is that backup code? I need a little help here!"
The Betrayal Revealed: The heroes realize the truth instantly. Varth didn't send Okeefe to secure the ship; he sent her there to be destroyed along with the evidence. He views her as a loose end. When the heroes confront him or hesitate, Varth sighs, sounding annoyed rather than guilty.
"She knew the risks. Look, if we turn back, that Star Destroyer will range us. We have the money. We have the Leviathan data. We jump. Now."
Scene 6: The Choice The heroes must decide: Follow Varth and leave Okeefe to die, or turn and fight. Assuming they choose to act as heroes:
The Dogfight: The heroes (in the Glorious Chariot and Z-95s) must engage the TIE Fighters screening the Gas Hauler.
The Objective: Destroy the Tibanna Gas Hauler before it impacts the slave ship. It is a massive target with heavy shielding, requiring concentrated fire.
The "Varth Intervention": As the heroes engage the TIEs, they find themselves outgunned. TIEs swarm their position. Just as shields are failing, laser fire erupts from the flank, vaporizing a TIE fighter on a hero's tail.
It is Varth. He has brought their original ship around for an attack run. He is furious.
"Blast it all! I am not losing my 50% cut because you idiots decided to be martyrs! I am engaging the TIEs. You focus on that hauler! And I am billing you for the fuel!"
GM Note: This preserves Varth’s cover. He isn't saving them out of altruism; he is saving them because they are the only ones who can retrieve the fortune for him. He needs them alive—for now.
The Climax: With Varth holding off the TIEs, the heroes pour fire into the Tibanna Gas Hauler. It explodes in a massive fireball just meters from the slave ship.
Okeefe: "That's it! Slave circuits fried. Nav-computer is green. We are jumping to the rendezvous... and Varth? You and I are going to have a very long talk."
The Shackles of Nizon vanishes into hyperspace.
Conclusion: The Escape The Assiduous begins to fire turbolasers, green bolts flashing past the heroes' cockpits. Varth screams over the comms:
"We overstayed our welcome! Punch it!"
The heroes, Varth, and the stolen fighters jump to hyperspace.
Aftermath (End of Act I) The heroes arrive at a safe rendezvous point.
The Confrontation: Okeefe is furious, punching Varth in the jaw. Varth takes it, smoothing his jacket and claiming it was a "tactical calculation that changed."
The Rewards:
The Ship: The heroes now possess the Glorious Chariot (a Luxury 3000) and potentially Z-95 fighters.
The Credits: They have the tournament winnings and Darga’s liquid assets.
The Data: They have the Project Leviathan files.
The Tease: Varth wipes the blood from his lip and looks at the datapad.

 "This isn't just a smuggling operation. This data points to a massive construction project. But it's not in the Core. It's in the Unknown Regions. If you want to know what the Empire is really building, we have to go off the map."
