As Astra Kynetus, I am locking onto the psychological and mechanical levers that drive player behavior. 

If we want a budget of **10 to 15 Marks per adventure**, we cannot just hand them out at the end of a session. Players need a menu of narrative and mechanical triggers. They need to know that *leaning into the danger* is what prints the currency.

To prevent farming (especially that D4 Frail Sage exploding 25% of the time) while ensuring everyone can reasonably hit the baseline of 10 Marks, we must divide the Mark triggers into four distinct buckets: **The Mission, The Past, The Future, and The Mechanics.**

Here is the complete **Fulcrum Mark Generation System**, perfectly math-balanced for a 10-15 Mark payout.

---

### **BUCKET 1: THE MISSION (The Shared Struggle)**
*Budget: 4 to 5 Marks per Adventure*
These are the closest things to "automatic" Marks, but they are tied strictly to story progression and surviving the Game Master's overarching narrative. 
* **The Act Milestone (1 Mark):** Awarded when the crew successfully completes an Act or major objective within the adventure. *(In a 3-Act adventure, this generates 3 Marks).*
* **The Crucible (1 Mark):** Awarded when the crew survives a catastrophic setback, a terrifying boss encounter, or a brutal narrative twist. 
* **The Hard Call (1 Mark):** Awarded as a group when the crew makes a definitive, galaxy-altering choice (e.g., choosing to save the data drive instead of the hostages, or betraying a contact to survive). 

### **BUCKET 2: THE PAST (The Phase Trio Triggers)**
*Budget: 2 to 3 Marks per Adventure*
Fulcrum characters are built on a Phase Trio (their history before the game). These Marks are earned when a player allows their past to complicate their present.
* **The Ghost of the Past (1 Mark):** Awarded when a player intentionally uses a detail from their Phase 1, 2, or 3 background to complicate the current mission. *(e.g., "The Imperial officer inspecting our ship is the same one who raided my village in Phase 2. I'm going to lose my cool.")*
* **The Debt Paid (1 Mark):** Awarded when a character goes out of their way, at personal risk, to honor a bond or protect a crewmate established in their shared backstory.
* **The Old Scars (1 Mark):** Awarded when a player intentionally chooses to fail a social or mental check because their background dictates they have PTSD, a phobia, or a deep-seated bias regarding the current situation. 

### **BUCKET 3: THE FUTURE (Destiny Triggers)**
*Budget: 2 to 3 Marks per Adventure*
Every character has a driving Destiny—the reason they are risking their lives in the Dark Times instead of keeping their head down. 
* **The Reckless Pursuit (1 Mark):** Awarded when a character takes a highly unoptimal or dangerous action during a scene specifically because it inches them closer to their Destiny. *(e.g., Breaking stealth to access a terminal that might have information on their missing sister).*
* **The Destiny Milestone (2 Marks):** Awarded when the character achieves a tangible, permanent step toward fulfilling their ultimate Destiny goal. *(This is rare, and should feel like a massive character moment).*

### **BUCKET 4: THE MECHANICS (The Action Triggers)**
*Budget: 2 to 4 Marks per Adventure*
This is where we integrate the Fulcrum engine's specific mechanics (Arenas, Edge, and Favored Disciplines). We heavily gate these to prevent players from "farming" dice mechanics.
* **The D4 Burden (1 Mark - Max 2 per Adv):** A hero is defined by their flaws. If a player willingly chooses to automatically fail a roll tied to their D4 Arena—narrating how their physical frailty, lack of charm, or terrible reflexes ruins the moment—they earn a Mark. *(This makes taking a D4 incredibly lucrative, but heavily damages the immediate scene).*
* **The Unleashed Miracle (1 Mark - Max 1 per Adv):** We do not award Marks *every* time a Favored Discipline explodes (otherwise D4s would break the economy). Instead, this Mark is awarded when a player goes Unleashed at the absolute most dramatic moment, turning a guaranteed failure into a Flawless, scene-ending victory. 
* **The Edge Burn (1 Mark):** Awarded when a character voluntarily burns their Edge pool to absolute 0 to fuel a desperate Gambit or Maneuver to save another crewmate from a fatal blow. 

---

### **The Architect's Payout Analysis: Does the Math Work?**

Let’s look at a standard player going through a 3-Act Fulcrum Adventure using this checklist. 

**The "Quiet" Player (The Baseline):**
* Survives Acts 1, 2, and 3: **+3 Marks**
* Survives the Act 2 Crucible (The Boss/Setback): **+1 Mark**
* Helps make The Hard Call in Act 3: **+1 Mark**
* Uses a Phase Trio bond to help a friend once: **+1 Mark**
* Explodes an Unleashed roll to win a fight: **+1 Mark**
* Takes a risk for their Destiny: **+1 Mark**
* **Total Earned:** **8 to 9 Marks.** *(They didn't roleplay heavily, but they survived and engaged with the mechanics. They earn enough to buy a few cheap advances).*

**The "Cinematic" Roleplayer (The Maximum):**
* Hits all the Story/Mission milestones: **+5 Marks**
* Lets their Phase 2 background ruin a stealth check: **+1 Mark**
* Honors a Phase Trio bond to save a friend: **+1 Mark**
* Intentionally fails a roll because of their D4 Physique: **+1 Mark**
* Recklessly breaks cover to steal Destiny data: **+1 Mark**
* Achieves a major Destiny Milestone: **+2 Marks**
* Burns their Edge to 0 to save the squad: **+1 Mark**
* Lands an Unleashed Miracle on the final boss: **+1 Mark**
* **Total Earned:** **13 to 14 Marks.** *(They actively drove the story, leaned into their flaws, and created cinematic chaos. They are richly rewarded).*

### **Why This is Brilliant for the GM:**
Because of this checklist, the Game Master never has to arbitrarily decide "Did they roleplay well enough to level up?" 

The players hold the checklist. The players *want* the Marks to afford their D12 Apex abilities. Therefore, the players will *intentionally* trigger their own background flaws, willingly fail their D4 checks, and recklessly pursue their Destinies. **They will build the drama for you.**
