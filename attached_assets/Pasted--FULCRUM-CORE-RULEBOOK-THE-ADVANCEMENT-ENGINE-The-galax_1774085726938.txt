***FULCRUM CORE RULEBOOK: THE ADVANCEMENT ENGINE***

*“The galaxy doesn’t hand out medals for surviving another day in the Outer Rim. You don’t get better by just breathing the recycled air of a safehouse. You get better by bleeding, by pushing your luck until it shatters, and by looking your Destiny in the eye when the blaster bolts start flying.”*

In **Fulcrum**, characters do not simply "level up." Growth is a grueling, tactical, and narrative-driven process. The characters are flawed, desperate individuals navigating the Imperial Dark Times. Becoming a legend requires sacrifice, focus, and knowing exactly when to rely on your raw talent versus your specialized training. 

Here is the complete mechanical architecture of the Fulcrum Advancement Engine.

---

### **I. THE CURRENCY OF SURVIVAL: MARKS**
Progression in Fulcrum is fueled by **Marks**. Marks are the narrative scars, hard-won lessons, and pivotal moments of clarity a character achieves during a campaign. 

* **No Participation Trophies:** You do not earn Marks just for showing up to the table. 
* **Earning Marks:** Marks are awarded dynamically for hitting specific triggers: completing personal **Destiny Goals**, reaching major **Story Milestones** defined by the Game Master, or leaning heavily into the chaos of the system (such as surviving the volatile fallout of an **Unleashed** Discipline roll). 
* **Spending Marks:** Marks are a banked resource. They can only be spent to purchase **Advances** during narrative downtime—a break in the action where the crew can catch their breath, repair their gear, and reflect on their survival.

---

### **II. THE TOLL OF EXPERIENCE: THE ESCALATING TRACKS**
The core philosophy of Fulcrum’s progression is that fixing a glaring flaw is relatively easy, but achieving true perfection is an agonizingly steep climb. 

Progression is managed through **Tracks**. As a character completes a Track, they move to the next one, and the Mark cost to purchase an Advance increases. This simulates the plateau of mastery; a veteran must push themselves much harder to see the same level of improvement as a rookie.

There are three distinct avenues of progression: **Disciplines, Arenas, and Vocations.**

---

### **III. HONING THE EDGE: DISCIPLINE ADVANCEMENT**
Disciplines are your skills (Piloting, Shoot, Mechanics, etc.). They represent your horizontal versatility.

**The Discipline Track**
The Discipline Track is 5 boxes long. Purchasing 1 box equals **1 Discipline Advance**.
* **Track 1:** 1 Mark per Advance (Total 5 Marks to clear).
* **Track 2:** 2 Marks per Advance (Total 10 Marks to clear).
* **Track 3:** 3 Marks per Advance (Total 15 Marks to clear).
* *And so on.*

**Stepping Up the Die**
* Spending **1 Advance** increases any single Discipline by one die step (e.g., D6 to D8). 
* **The Soft Cap:** Standard Advances can only push a Discipline to a maximum of **D8**.

**Breaking the Cap: Elite Tokens**
When a player fills the 5th box of their current Discipline Track, they earn **1 Elite Token**. These tokens represent profound breakthroughs in technique and are required to push past the D8 soft cap.
* **D8 to D10:** Costs 1 Advance + **1 Elite Token**.
* **D10 to D12 (Apex):** Costs 1 Advance + **2 Elite Tokens**.
*(To reach a D12 in a single Discipline, a character must earn 3 Elite Tokens, which requires completing 15 total Discipline Advances).*

**The Focus Burn (The Specialist's Sacrifice)**
If a player wishes to reach an Elite Token quickly without diluting their character concept by upgrading skills they don't care about, they may declare a **Focus Burn**. They pay the Mark cost for their current Track to check off a box, but they intentionally *do not* upgrade a Discipline. They sacrifice broad versatility to accelerate their path toward vertical mastery.

---

### **IV. FORGING THE FLESH: ARENA ADVANCEMENT**
Arenas (Physique, Reflex, Grit, Wits, Presence) are a character's core attributes. They are incredibly powerful because they dictate the character's global **Edge pool maximum** (D4 = 1 Edge, D12 = 5 Edge). Because they alter the action economy, they are expensive to upgrade.

**The Arena Track**
The Arena Track is separated from Disciplines and consists of 3 boxes per tier. The cost of an Arena Advance is strictly calculated as: `Track Level × 3 Marks`.
* **Track 1:** 3 Marks per Advance.
* **Track 2:** 6 Marks per Advance.
* **Track 3:** 9 Marks per Advance.
* *And so on.*

**The Cost of Perfection**
Unlike Disciplines, stepping up an Arena often requires spending multiple Advances at once, simulating the sheer physical or mental toll of fundamental self-improvement:
* **Fixing a Flaw (D4 to D6):** Costs **2 Advances** *(A heavy penalty for neglecting a core stat).*
* **Standard Upgrade (D6 to D8):** Costs **1 Advance**.
* **Elite Upgrade (D8 to D10):** Costs **3 Advances**.
* **Master Upgrade (D10 to D12):** Costs **5 Advances**.

**The Apex Rule**
No hero is a god in all things. A character may only ever possess **one Arena at a D12**. If a character wishes to push a different Arena from a D10 to a D12 later in their career, they must pay the 5 Advances to do so—and their previous D12 Arena immediately degrades back to a D10. 

---

### **V. MASTERING THE PATH: VOCATION ADVANCEMENT**
Vocations are your specialized toolkits—the Gambits, Maneuvers, and Exploits that define your role in the galaxy. Every Vocation has 5 Tiers of abilities.

**The Discipline Gate**
You cannot buy tactical expertise you lack the foundational skill to execute. A Vocation’s maximum allowed Tier is strictly gated by the character’s current die size in that Vocation’s **Favored Discipline**:
* **D4:** Max Tier 1
* **D6:** Max Tier 2
* **D8:** Max Tier 3
* **D10:** Max Tier 4
* **D12:** Max Tier 5

**Unlocking Tiers**
If a character meets the Favored Discipline requirement, they may spend Marks to unlock the next Tier in their Vocation. The cost mirrors the escalating weight of the Arena system (`Tier Level × 3 Marks`):
* **Unlock Tier 2:** 6 Marks
* **Unlock Tier 3:** 9 Marks
* **Unlock Tier 4:** 12 Marks
* **Unlock Tier 5 (Apex Ability):** 15 Marks

---

### **VI. THE CINEMATIC ARC (Summary)**
In a standard Fulcrum campaign, a character will earn roughly **100 to 150 Marks** over their lifetime. 

Because of the escalating track costs, a player mathematically *cannot* max out every stat. Reaching the "Holy Trinity" of a build—one D12 Arena, one D12 Discipline, and one Tier 5 Vocation—will consume almost 90 Marks. 

This forces agonizing, brilliant choices. Do you hoard your Marks to become an untouchable Tier 5 sniper, leaving your *Physique* at a frail D4 (giving you only 1 Edge to survive physical trauma)? Or do you panic-spend your hard-earned Marks mid-campaign to broaden your skills and survive the horrors of the galaxy, delaying your ultimate mastery until the final, climactic act of your story? 

In Fulcrum, you are what you survive. Choose your advances carefully.
