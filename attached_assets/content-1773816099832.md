" _You both shall learn. When you face one Nightsister of Dathomir...you face us all!_"―Merrin, to [Taron Malicos](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Taron_Malicos "Taron Malicos") and [Cal Kestis](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Cal_Kestis "Cal Kestis")[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13)

The **Nightsisters**, also known as the **Witches of Dathomir** or the **Nightsister acolytes**, were a [clan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomiri_clan "Dathomiri clan") and ancient order of [magick](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Magick "Magick")-wielding [women](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Gender "Gender"), most of whom were [Dathomirian](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") [Zabraks](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Zabrak "Zabrak"), who lived on [Dathomir](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"), a [planet](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Planet "Planet") bathed in [dark energies](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"). These dark side users were able to perform their arcane magicks by tapping into the [magical ichor](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_ichor "Magical ichor") that flowed from the depths of their planet.

## History\[ [Edit section: History](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

### Life on Dathomir\[ [Edit section: Life on Dathomir](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Life on Dathomir")\]

According to a commonly told story, the first of[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6) the ancient[\[14\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Dathomir-14) Nightsisters were trained in the ways of [the Force](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Force "The Force") by [Allya](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Allya "Allya"), a [female](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Gender "Gender") [Jedi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") whom the [Jedi Council](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi_Council "Jedi Council") had sent into [exile](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)") on the mysterious [planet](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Dathomir](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"). However, the Jedi Council had no records of an exile named Allya, and the Nightsisters themselves had other, contradicting tales about their origins. Even if she was not the founder of the sisters,[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6) it was true that Allya wrote the [Book of Law](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Book_of_Law "Book of Law"), which taught the sisters to dislike the Jedi.[\[7\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-BMF_59-7) However, the origins of the [Dathomirian](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") people actually lied on the [extragalactic](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Extragalactic "Extragalactic") planet [Peridea](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Peridea "Peridea"),[\[15\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-CompEncyc-15) which was once the site of the [Witch Kingdom of the Dathmiri](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_Kingdom_of_the_Dathmiri "Witch Kingdom of the Dathmiri"). As their original [homeworld](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld"), the ancient Dathomiri tamed the [Purrgil](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Purrgil "Purrgil"), beings that could traverse [hyperspace](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace") and therefore bridge the gap between [Peridea's galaxy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Far_galaxy "Far galaxy") and [Dathomir's galaxy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"). The [Great Mothers](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Mothers "Great Mothers"),[\[10\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Far_Far_Away-10) three Nightsisters[\[16\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Aktropaw_Cutout-16) who slumbered on Peridea, hated the Jedi as well.[\[17\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_Eight-17)

When the Nightsisters eventually discovered the planet they would name Dathomir, done so in honor of their ancient Dathomiri origins, they crossed paths with its inhabitants, the [Fromprath](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Fromprath "Fromprath"),[\[15\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-CompEncyc-15) who were exploiting the natural resources of the planet. Opposing the technological society[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18) in order to claim the planet from the Fromprath,[\[15\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-CompEncyc-15) the Nightsisters formed a symbiotic alliance with the [rancors](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rancor "Rancor") and mounted them in combat.[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18) In the end, the Nightsisters managed to kick the Fromprath off Dathomir,[\[15\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-CompEncyc-15) forcing them into exile.[\[19\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-LS-19) Knowing that technologically advanced aliens posed a threat to [covens](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomiri_clan "Dathomiri clan"), the Nightsisters modernized some of their weapons, such as their [energy bows](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsister_energy_bow "Nightsister energy bow").[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18)

According to legend, the Nightsister [Zeldin](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Zeldin "Zeldin") went after the [Sith](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Sith "Sith") [Darth Caldoth](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Darth_Caldoth "Darth Caldoth"), after he stole a [burial pod](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Burial_pod "Burial pod") from Dathomir. Zeldin attempted to manipulate Caldoth into causing his own death out of revenge, but Caldoth was aware of Zeldin's presence, exploiting their connection before trapping her consciousness within his mind.[\[20\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Gilded_Cage-20) Though a weapon used more commonly by [Jedi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), the Nightsisters had adopted [lightwhips](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Lightwhip "Lightwhip") by the [High Republic Era](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era").[\[21\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-A_Test_of_Courage-21) In [232 BBY](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/232_BBY "232 BBY"),[\[22\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-THRdatenote-22) [Jedi Padawan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Imri Cantaros](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Imri_Cantaros "Imri Cantaros") thought about how the Nightsisters had less rules than the Jedi and their creative, more destructive values.[\[21\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-A_Test_of_Courage-21)

A period took place known as the [Golden Age of the Nightsisters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Golden_Age_of_the_Dathomiri_Nightsisters "Golden Age of the Dathomiri Nightsisters"). The clan flourished and the separate Nightsister clans lived in harmony. However, the era nearly came to an end as the clans drifted apart. However, the iron will of new [Clan Mother](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Talzin "Talzin") kept the clans together,[\[23\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Bespin_and_Other_Outer_Rim_Planets-23) unifying them[\[24\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Mother_Talzin_card-24) into a [single clan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mother_Talzin%27s_clan "Mother Talzin's clan").[\[2\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre-2) During this time, Talzin worked alongside [Sith Lord](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious").[\[25\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-SonOfDathomir3-25) While she became wary of such machinations, knowing there could only be two powerful dark side factions at one time due to the [Rule of Two](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two"),[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18) Sidious promised she would be his right hand. Both shared secret knowledge and worked to combine dark side powers and Nightsister magicks. Ultimately, however, he betrayed Talzin by taking her oldest son [Maul](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Maul "Maul") from her to train as his [Sith apprentice](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice").[\[25\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-SonOfDathomir3-25) At the time of the [Clone Wars](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), Talzin began to sell her sisters' services as [mercenaries](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary") to [the galaxy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy")'s wealthy citizens.[\[26\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-sweMT-26)

[![Asajj Ventress returns home to Dathomir.](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f4/WelcomeHomeAsajj-Nightsisters.png/revision/latest?cb=20130521035415)

Asajj Ventress returns home to Dathomir.

In [50 BBY](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/50_BBY "50 BBY"),[\[27\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Asajj_Birth_Date-27) the clan was forced to give up the young [Asajj Ventress](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress") to the [Siniteen](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Siniteen "Siniteen") criminal [Hal'Sted](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Hal%27Sted "Hal'Sted") as insurance for protection.[\[8\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-tcwNS-8)

Other witch groups active on Dathomir included the [Singing Mountain](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Singing_Mountain_(clan) "Singing Mountain (clan)") and [Blue Coral Divers Clan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Blue_Coral_Divers_Clan "Blue Coral Divers Clan"). A couple years before the [Clone](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") Wars, Talzin came to tell the witch of no clan [Falta](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Falta "Falta") to give up her daughter, [Yenna](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Yenna "Yenna"). After Falta declined, Yenna slowly became frustrated with her mother's teachings, and one day left to join the Nightsisters in hopes of different teaching.[\[28\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Bug-28) The Nightsisters became embroiled in [Arzo Suun](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Arzo_Suun "Arzo Suun")'s [attempt to steal from a Nightbrother village](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Attempt_to_steal_from_a_Nightbrother_village "Attempt to steal from a Nightbrother village").[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6)

During the Clone Wars, some of the mysterious Nightsisters supported [Count](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") and the [Separatists](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"), utilizing their "magick" to help their allies. However, the Nightsisters were always on their own side, and any such partnerships are inevitably temporary.[\[4\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Dathomir-4) At some point, [Jedi Knights](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and [Anakin Skywalker](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") [investigated](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Investigation_on_Rodia "Investigation on Rodia") into the actions of some rogue Nightsisters on [Rodia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rodia "Rodia").[\[29\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Mustafar_and_Other-29)

### Downfall and collapse\[ [Edit section: Downfall and collapse](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Downfall and collapse")\]

[![The Battle of Dathomir resulted in the decimation of the Nightsisters clan.](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/BattleOfDathomir-Massacre.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20120225171355)

The Battle of Dathomir resulted in the decimation of the Nightsisters clan.

Ventress later returned to her people after being betrayed by [Count](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Count_of_Serenno "Count of Serenno") Dooku, seeking their help for revenge against her former Master.[\[8\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-tcwNS-8) After the failure of their first attempt, the Nightsisters used the [Nightbrother](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightbrothers "Nightbrothers") and Talzin’s younger son and Maul’s brother [Savage Opress](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Savage_Opress "Savage Opress") as an instrument to destroy Dooku. They used their magic to increase his size, rage, and power.[\[30\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Monster-30) However, their scheme would earn them the ire of Dooku, who would later dispatch [General](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/General "General") [Grievous](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Grievous "Grievous") to Dathomir in retaliation.[\[2\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre-2) The witches also posed a threat to Sidious' new evil [Empire](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire")[\[31\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Visions_and_Voices-31) and thus were a target he wanted destroyed.[\[32\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-SonOfDathomir1-32) Grievous then destroyed their fortress and killed off the majority of the Nightsisters during the [battle](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Dathomir "Battle of Dathomir"),[\[2\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre-2) marking the end of the Nightsisters as a civilization.[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13) The few survivors included Ventress, ### Survival and adapting\[ [Edit section: Survival and adapting](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Survival and adapting")\]

" _I've spent years…of waiting for a chance to avenge my sisters. I'm finished waiting. I wish to fight by your side. Nightsisters and Jedi do not travel together but…survivors. We adapt._"―Merrin, to Cal Kestis[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13)

[![Survivors such as Merrin were among the last of the Nightsisters.](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/77/JFO_Nightsister_Merrin_introduction.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191124143113)

Following the [end](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar") of the Clone Wars, the Nightsisters' massacre at the hands of Grievous and Dooku became known, with very few Nightsisters remaining, like Merrin. However, as the Nightbrothers weren't wiped out. Merrin would take them as her servants, and they took control of Dathomir during the [age](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") of the Empire.[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13)

Although Grievous's attack marked the end of the Nightsisters,[\[38\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-DD-38)[\[31\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Visions_and_Voices-31) there existed clans of Force users on Dathomir by the time of the Imperial Era who continued to use the name, such as the sect known as the [Nardithi Nightsisters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nardithi_Nightsisters "Nardithi Nightsisters"). These groups remained a concern for Sidious, who ordered a small [garrison](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Garrison "Garrison") of the [Imperial Military](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military") to report if any tried to make it off world. At some point, a female [Inquisitor](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius") found [Jerserra](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jerserra "Jerserra"), a member of the Nardithi Nightsisters, and took her as an apprentice. However, years later, Jerserra betrayed her master, covered up her death, and took her [double-bladed spinning lightsaber](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Double-bladed_spinning_lightsaber "Double-bladed spinning lightsaber").[\[4\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Dathomir-4) In time, the Nardithi Nightsisters and other such groups would no longer be found on Dathomir.[\[42\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Dawn_note-42) Legends claim that the spirits of the slaughtered Nightsisters still haunt the swamps of their miserable world.[\[43\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Secrets_of_the_Sith-43)

Elsbeth was enraged by the massacre of her people during the Clone Wars. In her rage, she built up the [Imperial Navy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Imperial_Navy "Imperial Navy") by plundering worlds of their resources in order to get power to secure her future to take revenge on her enemies.[\[44\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Chapter_13-44) In [9 ABY](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/9_ABY "9 ABY"),[\[45\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Date-45) former [Jedi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [Padawan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Ahsoka Tano](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") recounted the story of Elsbeth's people to the [Mandalorian](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mandalorian "Mandalorian") [bounty hunter](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Din Djarin](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Din_Djarin "Din Djarin") in [9 ABY](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/9_ABY "9 ABY").[\[44\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Chapter_13-44)

In [14 BBY](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/14_BBY "14 BBY"),[\[46\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFOdatenote-46) Merrin [joined](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mission_to_Dathomir_(Cal_Kestis) "Mission to Dathomir (Cal Kestis)") the [crew](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Stinger_Mantis_crew "Stinger Mantis crew") of the [Stinger Mantis](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Stinger_Mantis "Stinger Mantis"), made up of [Jedi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") Knight [Cal Kestis](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Cal_Kestis "Cal Kestis"), former Knight [Cere Junda](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Cere_Junda "Cere Junda"), the [latero](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Latero "Latero") pilot Captain [Greez Dritus](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Greez_Dritus "Greez Dritus"), and the droid [BD-1](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/BD-1 "BD-1"), with whom she left Dathomir to explore the galaxy.[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13)

Working together, Djarin and Tano [liberated Calodan](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Liberation_of_Calodan "Liberation of Calodan") and Tano demanded her to reveal where Thrawn was.[\[44\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Chapter_13-44) Elsbeth was then taken into the custody of the New Republic, where she revealed the location of an ancient [temple](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters%27_Temple "Nightsisters' Temple") built by her people on [Arcana](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Arcana "Arcana") containing a map to [Peridea](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Peridea "Peridea"),[\[33\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_One-33) a planet in a [distant galaxy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Far_galaxy "Far galaxy") which was the ancient homeworld of the Nightsisters,[\[50\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_Two-50) and the place of exile for Thrawn. The map was recovered by Tano and unlocked by Wren. Elsbeth was freed from New Republic imprisonment by former Jedi Knight [Baylan Skoll](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Baylan_Skoll "Baylan Skoll") and his apprentice [Shin Hati](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Hati "Shin Hati"), and the map was taken from Sabine in a duel with Shin.[\[33\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_One-33) Using her influence with Imperial loyalists at her former [shipyard](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Facility_301G "Facility 301G") on [Corellia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Corellia "Corellia"), Elsbeth had ordered the construction of a massive [hyperspace transport ring](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Hyperspace_transport_ring "Hyperspace transport ring") called the _[Eye of Sion](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Eye_of_Sion "Eye of Sion")_, which was completed shortly after her escape from the New Republic.[\[50\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_Two-50)

Morgan and her allies used the Eye to jump to Peridea with coordinates gained from the map, using a [reflex point](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Seatos_reflex_point "Seatos reflex point") on the planet [Seatos](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Seatos "Seatos").[\[51\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_Four-51) At Peridea, Elsbeth and Thrawn were reunited at a [Nightsister fortress](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Mothers%27_Fortress "Great Mothers' Fortress") and it was revealed he allied himself with the Dathomiri [Great Mothers](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Mothers "Great Mothers"). ## Religion and philosophy\[ [Edit section: Religion and philosophy](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Religion and philosophy")\]

" _Nightsisters know the dark side better than anyone. We grow up steeped in it, but we can use it as a tool and stay ourselves—unlike the Sith. That balance is what you must learn._"― [Asajj Ventress](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress"), to [Quinlan Vos](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Quinlan_Vos "Quinlan Vos")[\[38\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-DD-38)

[![Dathomir, a planet bathed in "magick"](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/Dathomir_DoR.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200529111732)

Dathomir, a planet bathed in "magick"

By tapping into the [magical ichor](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_ichor "Magical ichor") that flowed from Dathomir's depths, the witches could harness a power[\[52\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-sweDT-52) they referred to as " [magick](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Magick "Magick")."[\[53\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-sweNS-53) The most powerful of the Nightsisters could use that ichor to create objects out of thin air,[\[30\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Monster-30) transform people into ghostly versions of their true forms,[\[8\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-tcwNS-8) or even [reanimate the dead](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Army_of_the_Dead "Army of the Dead").[\[2\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre-2) They were also known to domesticate benign [rancors](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rancor "Rancor").[\[54\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Complete_Visual_Dictionary-54) Maul identified magick as an aspect of the Force.[\[31\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Visions_and_Voices-31)

Although the Jedi and Sith would view their powers as residing in the [dark side of the force](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), the Nightsisters' study differed from the power hungry ambitions of the Sith or the seeking of knowledge of the [Jedi Order](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). In actuality, they made little distinction between the [light](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") and the dark, though their techniques were frequently dark in nature. Nightsisters' viewed everything such as animals, weapons like the energy bows, and even the [Nightbrothers](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightbrothers "Nightbrothers") as tools to ensure their clan's ultimate survival and would bend or discard such tools without remorse, pity, and regret. Due to this mindset, they could avoid the passions and rage that swayed the Sith while wielding powers a Jedi withheld tapping into.[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6)

Some Nightsisters believed in the [Winged Goddess](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Winged_Goddess "Winged Goddess"), a deity of life, fertility, and divination, while some others believed in the [Fanged God](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Fanged_God "Fanged God"), a deity of death and the hunt. The Nightsisters did not celebrate death, but did not fear it and saw it as a part of life, with the divide being a veil that they would all eventually pass through.[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6)

## Society and culture\[ [Edit section: Society and culture](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Society and culture")\]

### Overview\[ [Edit section: Overview](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Overview")\]

" _I believe the old alliance can again be what it once was. You would only have to pledge your clan's allegiance to the Separatists' cause. We could certainly use the powers of the Nightsisters against the Jedi._"

" _Such a_ generous _offer, Count. But our loyalty is reserved for only each other._"―Count Dooku and Mother Talzin[\[30\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Monster-30)

[![Mother Talzin's clan](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/62/DaughtersOfDathomir-TSWB.png/revision/latest?cb=20251115005905)

Mother Talzin's clan

Most Nightsisters were [Dathomirian](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian"),[\[2\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre-2) but members of other species, such as[\[33\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_One-33) the [human](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human")[\[55\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-SWCEUAE-55) [Morgan Elsbeth](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Morgan_Elsbeth "Morgan Elsbeth"), could be part of the coven.[\[33\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_One-33) Several studies indicated that the Nightsisters were a peaceful tribe who lived in harmony with their planet, but this was not true.[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18) The Nightsisters of Dathomir lived in the seclusion of a stone fortress that bordered dense swamplands.[\[52\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-sweDT-52) A matriarchal society, the coven of sisters lived apart from the men of Dathomir, the [Nightbrothers](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightbrothers "Nightbrothers"), whose only use was to serve the sisters as servants and breeders.[\[53\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-sweNS-53) Nightsisters were known to have less rules than Jedi.[\[21\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-A_Test_of_Courage-21)

Nightsister positions included Nightsister Initiates, Nightsister Acolytes,[\[56\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-GoH-56) and the ruling Clan Mother.[\[30\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Monster-30) The Nightsisters mummified their dead before placing them in pods of animal skin decorated with tassels, which were hung on structures made of branches, [bones](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Bone "Bone"), animal skins, and shells. Their graveyards mimicked the configuration of the plant life seen on Dathomir, with its crooked [trees](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Tree "Tree") burdened by large, cocoon-like [fruits](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Fruit "Fruit").[\[57\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre_Trivia_Gallery-57)

### Nightsister funerals\[ [Edit section: Nightsister funerals](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Nightsister funerals")\]

Before placing [dead Nightsisters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsister_Zombie "Nightsister Zombie") in burial pods, Nightsisters went through many steps to prepare a [funeral](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Funeral "Funeral"). When using cloth for burial pods, the cloth was washed in [magickal](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Magick "Magick") [water](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water"). Then, the cloth was hand-braided together while Nightsisters recited a [spell of protection](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_of_protection "Spell of protection"). Finally, the bodies were cleansed with fragrant [oils](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Oil "Oil") and placed in the newly created burial pod[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13) and mummified.[\[57\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Massacre_Trivia_Gallery-57)[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6)

## Skills and training\[ [Edit section: Skills and training](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Skills and training")\]

Considered formidable force-wielders, the Nightsisters are known to wield different powers compared to other force wielders derived from incantations and spells. While rumors persisted about them being powerful, only strong leaders within the Nightsisters had power that could contend with fully trained [Jedi Masters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") or [Sith Lords](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord").[\[6\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Collapse_of_the_Republic-6)

As young initiates, Nightsister witches undergo training as children to master their power over magick.[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13) Young women aspiring to join the ranks of the Nightsisters first had to use the Dark side to subdue a powerful, strong-willed creature living in the depths of the pools in the village known as [the Sleeper](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Sleeper "The Sleeper"), and bring back a part of its body. The harvested remains would then be converted into the [Water of Life](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Water_of_Life "Water of Life"), a major component of Nightsister magicks. Any initiate who gave into their fear, such as [Talia's twin sister](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Talia%27s_twin_sister "Talia's twin sister"), would be dragged down into the depths and killed.[\[38\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-DD-38) An important component of training was that a Nightsister had to master their power without losing control and giving into the dark side completely.[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13)

Years before they were wiped out, the Nightsisters practiced their magick inside the [Witches Horn](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Witches_Horn "Witches Horn"), a cliff high above the cave of the [chirodactyl](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Chirodactyl "Chirodactyl") [Gorgara](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgara "Gorgara"). A Nightsister there made potions and elixirs. Some were medicine, while others were [poisons](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison").[\[13\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-JFO-13) The Nightsisters also used spells once intended to help plants and animals thrive, to create incantations of dark magic that gave supernatural strength to their best warriors, imbuing them with ichor.[\[18\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-TDS-18)

## Behind the scenes\[ [Edit section: Behind the scenes](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters?veaction=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Behind the scenes")\]

[![Nightsister concept art for Jedi Alliance](<Base64-Image-Removed>)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c0/Yansu_Grjak.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20081226134213)

Nightsister concept art for _Jedi Alliance_

The [Nightsisters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters/Legends "Nightsisters/Legends") were created by [Dave Wolverton](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dave_Wolverton "Dave Wolverton"), and first appeared in his [_Star Wars_ Legends](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") [1994](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/1994 "1994") novel _[The Courtship of Princess Leia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Courtship_of_Princess_Leia "The Courtship of Princess Leia")_.[\[58\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-CoPL-58) In Legends, they are depicted as a splinter faction of the native [Witches of Dathomir](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Witches_of_Dathomir "Witches of Dathomir"); descendants of the fallen Jedi [Allya](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Allya "Allya") and appeared in multiple Legends works. Wolverton himself noted in an interview that he sought to create a set of powerful female figures in the _[Star Wars](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")_ universe that had been dominated by men at the time, and expressed delight that the concept was picked up by [George Lucas](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") for his backstory of Darth Maul.[\[59\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-dwolver-59)

George Lucas later decided to integrate them in his TV series _[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")_ after he saw [Lucasfilm Animation Singapore](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Lucasfilm_Animation_Singapore "Lucasfilm Animation Singapore")'s use of the Nightsisters in the video game _[Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Jedi Alliance](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars:_Jedi_Alliance "Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Jedi Alliance")_ ( [2008](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/2008 "2008")), and how they were inspired by a concept art by [Iain McCaig](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Iain_McCaig "Iain McCaig") for [_The Phantom Menace_](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"). They were first introduced in the current canon with the twelfth episode of the [third season](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_Three "The Clone Wars: Season Three"), " [Nightsisters](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters_(episode) "Nightsisters (episode)")," aired on [January 7](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/January_7 "January 7"), [2011](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/2011 "2011").[\[60\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Twitter_1-60) [Tricia Barr](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Tricia_Barr "Tricia Barr"), author of the [2018](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/2018 "2018") reference book _[Star Wars: The Dark Side](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Dark_Side "Star Wars: The Dark Side")_, extrapolated from source material that the Nightsisters had enough magick and technology to reproduce without the necessity of the Nightbrothers.[\[61\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Twitter_2-61)

Nightsisters—including the standard Nightsister, identified as a Nightsister acolyte—appeared as playable characters in the game _[Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galaxy_of_Heroes "Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes")_.[\[56\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-GoH-56) Morgan Elsbeth first appeared in " [Chapter 13: The Jedi](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Chapter_13:_The_Jedi "Chapter 13: The Jedi")," where she was played by [Diana Lee Inosanto](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Diana_Lee_Inosanto "Diana Lee Inosanto") and it was stated her "people" were slaughtered in the Clone Wars.[\[44\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Chapter_13-44) Inosanto's hairstylist, [Maria Sandoval](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Maria_Sandoval "Maria Sandoval"), claimed on her Instagram that Elsbeth's people were the Nightsisters.[\[62\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Sandoval_Instagram-62) This was confirmed in " [Part One: Master and Apprentice](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Part_One:_Master_and_Apprentice "Part One: Master and Apprentice")."[\[33\]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Nightsisters#cite_note-Part_One-33)
